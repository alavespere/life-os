# Signals for Delays — Construction Project Risk Indicators
_Source: Signals for Delays.docx — Project Performance Accelerator Initiative_
_Routed: 2026-04-07_

---

## Overview
A framework of leading indicators that predict construction project delays before they become schedule failures.

---

## Signal Categories

### Financial Signals
- Subcontractor payment delinquencies (slow payment from GC = cash flow stress downstream)
- Lien filings and lien waivers outstanding
- Material supplier invoice aging
- Bonding capacity changes or bond claims

### Workforce / Labor Signals
- Labor turnover on site
- Subcontractor crew size reductions
- Overtime spikes followed by abrupt crew reductions
- Key personnel departures

### Schedule / Performance Signals
- Milestone slippage in early phases (early slippage compounds)
- RFI volume spikes (design coordination failures = downstream delays)
- Submittal log backlog (material procurement risk)
- Weather day tracking vs. baseline assumptions

### Procurement / Supply Chain Signals
- Material lead time extensions (especially steel, electrical, mechanical)
- Single-source dependencies without backup suppliers
- Import/tariff exposure on key materials

### Behavioral / Relationship Signals
- Increased change order disputes
- Reduced GC communication frequency
- Owner payment delays to GC (cash flow stress at top of chain flows down)
- Subcontractor turnover mid-project

---

## BuildSafe IQ Application
These signals are the data inputs for the BuildSafe IQ risk intelligence engine. Each category maps to a data source:
- Financial signals → lien filings, credit data, bonding records
- Labor signals → payroll data, workforce analytics
- Schedule signals → project management system integrations
- Procurement signals → supply chain databases, commodity indices
- Behavioral signals → communication pattern analysis, dispute records
