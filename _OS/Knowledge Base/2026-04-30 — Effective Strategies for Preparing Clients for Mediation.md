# Effective Strategies for Preparing Clients for Mediation

**Source:** Greenspoon Marder LLP (Chad J. Tamaroff) | **Date:** April 30, 2026

## Core Framework
Mediation succeeds when clients arrive informed, organized, and emotionally ready. Attorney's role: demystify the process, align expectations, and equip clients to make sound decisions.

## Key Preparation Steps

**1. Set Realistic Expectations**
- Mediation is problem-solving, not trial — goal is mutually acceptable resolution, not total victory
- Define settlement ranges, walk-away points, and who has authority to settle before the session
- Translate risk into concrete terms: timeline costs, confidentiality preserved, business objectives

**2. Explain the Process**
- Walk through: pre-mediation exchanges → opening sessions → private caucuses → back-and-forth offers
- Prepare client for mediator techniques: reality testing, devil's advocacy questions, shuttle diplomacy
- Address confidentiality protections upfront

**3. Gather and Organize Documents**
- Core contracts, amendments, correspondence
- Damages analyses with clear methodologies
- Chronologies and cast-of-characters summaries
- Prior settlement communications and offers
- Prepare a concise mediation statement

**4. Address Emotional Readiness**
- Coach client on active listening, concise messaging, separating risk evaluation from emotional reactions
- Pre-plan de-escalation steps and breaks if needed

**5. Resolve Authority and Logistics Beforehand**
- Confirm full settlement authority and who signs
- Pre-draft term sheets covering: releases scope, no-admission language, tax characterization, confidentiality, non-disparagement, enforcement mechanisms

**6. Manage Information Strategy**
- Decide what to disclose, when, and how
- "Information trades" can unlock value without compromising trial posture
- Ensure consistency between client's oral narrative and documentary record

## Pre-Mediation Checklist
- [ ] Define objectives, walk-away points, authority
- [ ] Explain process, roles, confidentiality, timelines
- [ ] Assemble core documents, damages models, visuals
- [ ] Draft mediation summary
- [ ] Rehearse client narrative and opening remarks
- [ ] Pre-clear disclosure parameters and caucus strategy
- [ ] Pre-draft settlement agreement
- [ ] Align on emotional readiness and communication protocols
