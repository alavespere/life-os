# Continuous & Progressive Damage Exclusions
_Added: 2026-04-08_

---

## Overview

Some losses develop gradually over weeks, months, or even years. These are called **continuous and progressive damages**. Examples include water intrusion, mold, corrosion, or structural deterioration. There is no single triggering event — the damage builds quietly over time.

## The Coverage Problem

Insurance responds to **when damage occurs**, not when it is discovered. If damage starts before a policy begins, coverage may be limited or excluded altogether. That's why many policies include **continuous or progressive damage exclusions**.

## Key Takeaway

Early detection and early reporting can make all the difference.

- Document conditions at project start and at each phase
- Report suspected damage immediately — don't wait for confirmation
- Review policy language for C&P exclusions before placement
