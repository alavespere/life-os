### Continuous & Progressive Damage (captured external reference)
Some losses develop gradually over weeks, months, or even years. These are called continuous and progressive damages. Think water intrusion, mold, corrosion, or structural deterioration. There's no single event. The damage builds quietly over time. Insurance responds to when damage occurs, not when it's discovered. If damage starts before a policy begins, coverage may be limited or excluded altogether. That's why many policies include continuous or progressive damage exclusions. Bottom line: early detection and early reporting can make all the difference.
