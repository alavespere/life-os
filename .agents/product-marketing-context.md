It aggregates project performance data, financial signals, subcontractor history, and market conditions across 50+ data sources to surface risk before it materializes — giving commercial GCs, developers, and investors the intelligence they need to protect margins, protect relationships, and make better decisions.

**Product category:** Construction risk intelligence / construction tech

**Product type:** SaaS platform + embedded expert services

**Business model:** Annual SaaS subscriptions priced by construction volume + risk management services. Average ACV: $100K. Cash-positive at 19 clients. Subscription tiers:
- $100M/yr volume → $50K/yr

## Target Audience

**Target companies:** Commercial general contractors with $50M–$500M in annual construction volume. Multi-project operators in commercial, mixed-use, public sector, and institutional markets. Initial geographic focus: NH/New England, then FL, TX, Southern CA, Metro DC, Metro NY.

**Decision-makers:** CEO/Owner, CFO/Controller, Project Executive, Risk Manager

**Primary use case:** Identifying and managing project risk before it materializes into delays, cost overruns, subcontractor failures, or litigation.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Protect margin by catching risk signals 60–90 days before impact
- Qualify subcontractors using financial health and performance data (not just experience and relationships)
- Get enterprise-wide risk visibility across active project portfolio
- Move from reactive compliance to proactive risk management

**Use cases:**
- Subcontractor financial vetting and ongoing monitoring
- Real-time project health scoring (schedule risk, milestone slippage, RFI patterns)
- TCOR analysis — quantifying the full cost of risk beyond premium
- Pre-commitment GC and developer risk verification for investors and lenders

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** The signals that predict project failure exist — in lien filings, payment histories, subcontractor financials, permit data, market conditions. They arrive too late, in the wrong format, or in disconnected systems nobody is watching. By the time the problem is visible, the margin is already gone.

**Why current solutions fall short:**
- Procore handles project management, not risk intelligence
- Traditional brokers are reactive — they price last year's losses, not next year's risks
- Compliance tools (Billy, MyCOI) cover one slice of a 30-variable problem
- Data vendors hand you a spreadsheet — no expertise to interpret it
- No one has connected safety + financial + compliance + predictive intelligence

**What it costs them:**
- Average $300M contractor loses $42M annually in preventable losses
- 92% of all projects run over schedule
- 64% exceed budgets by an average of 28%
- TCOR is typically 3–5x the insurance premium line item alone

**Emotional tension:** The fear of being blindsided. The frustration of finding out what went wrong after the fact. The anxiety of managing $50M+ in active work with nothing but experience and gut feel.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors:** Platforms covering overlapping risk categories:
- **Procore** — project management with safety features; no financial or compliance intelligence; not a risk platform
- **Origami Risk** — enterprise risk management; not construction-specific; no predictive analytics or embedded expertise

**Secondary competitors:** Single-problem tools:
- **Billy Compliance / MyCOI** — certificate of insurance tracking only; no financial or performance data
- **Smartvid.io** — safety only (computer vision on job sites); no financial or market signals

**Indirect competitors:** The current approach most GCs use:
- **Traditional brokers (Lockton, Alliant, etc.)** — reactive placement, no predictive intelligence, no project-level monitoring
- **Gut feel + relationships** — "We know our subs" — works until it doesn't

**The gap:** No competitor has integrated all four dimensions — safety, financial, compliance, and predictive intelligence — in a single platform backed by embedded domain expertise.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:** (What drives them away from the status quo)
- Lost money on a risk event that in retrospect had clear signals
- Subcontractor failure on critical path after financial stress was already building
- TCOR increasing year-over-year without understanding why
- Owner or lender demanding documented risk management rigor they can't demonstrate

**Pull:** (What attracts them to BuildSafe IQ)
- Predictive intelligence vs. reactive response
- 8x ROI in Year One through avoided delays and cost overruns
- Single integrated view vs. three disconnected tools
- Team that has lived the problem (practitioner credibility, not vendor pitch)

**Habit:** (What keeps them stuck)
- "We've always relied on experience and relationships to judge risk"
- Existing Procore workflows and integrations
- Existing broker relationships (switching feels risky)
- "This is just how construction works"

**Anxiety:** (What worries them about switching)
- Implementation time and disruption to active projects
- Data security (project financials, sub relationships)
- "Another tool nobody uses"
- ROI uncertainty — hard to prove a loss that didn't happen

**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| TCOR | Total Cost of Risk — insurance premiums + direct losses + indirect losses + admin + taxes/fees. Always higher than GCs think. |
| Risk Intelligence | Aggregated, interpreted, actionable risk data — not raw data dumps |
| Leading indicators | Signals that precede a risk event: payment slowdowns, bonding capacity changes, schedule slippage patterns |
| ICP | Commercial GC, $50M–$500M annual volume, with active risk exposure |
| Embedded expertise | Domain expert review alongside the AI — we validate what the model flags |
