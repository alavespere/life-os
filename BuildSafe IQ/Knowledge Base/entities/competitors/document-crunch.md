# Document Crunch

AI-powered contract review platform for construction. Analyzes construction contracts, identifies risky clauses, and flags deviations from standard practice. Acquired by Trimble in 2025.

---

## What Document Crunch Does

- Ingests construction contracts (AIA, ConsensusDocs, custom)
- Flags risky language: indemnification clauses, limitation of liability provisions, notice requirements, payment terms
- Highlights deviations from owner-favorable vs. contractor-favorable positions
- Helps legal teams and project executives review contracts faster

**Their buyer:** Legal counsel, project executives, risk managers during contract negotiation.

---

## Critical Distinction from BSIQ

Josh Kanner's framing (May 2026): "Document Crunch is a risk management platform."

Correct reframe: **Document Crunch analyzes whether the contract language is risky. BSIQ analyzes whether the contractor can actually perform.**

| Dimension | Document Crunch | BSIQ |
|---|---|---|
| What is scored | Contract document language | Subcontractor capability and financial health |
| When used | Before signing / during negotiation | Before awarding scope / during project execution |
| Risk question answered | "Is this contract fair?" | "Will this sub default or cause a loss?" |
| Buyer | Legal counsel, project executive | Risk manager, CFO, insurance team |
| Data source | The contract itself | Financial statements, safety records, WIP, insurance history |

## The Trimble Acquisition Signal

Trimble acquired Document Crunch, validating that:
1. The market for AI-powered construction risk tools is real
2. Strategic buyers (not just VC) are acquiring in this space
3. Document Crunch's category (contract risk) is distinct from BSIQ's category (subcontractor performance risk)

Use this in pitch deck: Trimble acquisition validates AI risk tools; BSIQ addresses the dimension Document Crunch cannot — predictive subcontractor intelligence.
