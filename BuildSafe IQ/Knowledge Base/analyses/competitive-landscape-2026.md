# Competitive Landscape — Construction Risk Tech (Q2 2026)

Synthesized from Josh Kanner feedback (May 2026), market research, and oracle feeds. Use for Pitch Deck Slide 8 (Competitive Landscape) and Slide 15 (Why Now). Update when new funding rounds or acquisitions are announced.

---

## The Competitive Map

Three types of players exist in the "construction risk" space. BSIQ occupies a distinct position none of them hold.

| Company | What They Do | Who Buys It | What They Don't Do |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Procore + Datagrid** | Project management + AI workflow automation (submittals, RFIs) | Project Manager | Risk scoring; sub financial health; insurance intelligence |
| **Document Crunch** (Trimble) | Contract language analysis — flags risky clauses | Legal counsel, project executive | Predicts sub performance or default; no financial/safety analysis |
| **Zero RFI** | AI-enabled owner's rep service | Building owner / developer | Serves GC; no subcontractor risk scoring |
| **Newmetrix** (Oracle) | Construction safety/risk AI — now enterprise-only | Large enterprise construction programs | Available to mid-market GCs; acquired, no standalone product |
| **Cara AI** | Insurance workflow automation (COI, ACORD, email AI) | Broker/insurer back office | Risk scoring; GC-facing advisory |
| **BuildSafe IQ** | Subcontractor risk scoring + certified risk advisory service | GC Risk Manager, CFO, Insurance Team | — |

---

## BSIQ's Defensible White Space

**No one is answering the GC's core question:** "Will this sub default, cause a loss, or blow up my project?"

- Procore answers: "How is the project running?"
- Document Crunch answers: "Is the contract language risky?"
- Zero RFI answers: "Is my GC performing?" (for owners)
- Newmetrix answered this but is now enterprise-only inside Oracle
- BSIQ answers: "Should I award this sub? Will they perform?"

## Josh Kanner's Framing (Most Credible Voice in the Space)

Josh built and sold Newmetrix to Oracle. His read on the competitive landscape (May 2026):
- Current pitch "sounds like a copied platform" — pivot to tech-enabled service is correct
- TAM needs more credible sourcing
- Scope needs to narrow — "boiling the ocean" weakens the pitch
- AI/data science methodology needs to be shown, not just claimed

His willingness to continue the conversation after giving hard feedback = the pitch has a real thesis. He just needs to see execution clarity.
