# Positioning Rationale — Tech-Enabled Service vs. SaaS Platform

The decision to reframe BSIQ from "AI platform" to "tech-enabled construction risk advisory service." Triggered by Josh Kanner feedback (May 14, 2026). Directionally committed; final model not fully resolved with Ev.

---

## The Old Positioning (Pre-May 2026)

"BuildSafe IQ is an AI platform that scores subcontractor risk."

Josh Kanner's read: "Sounds like a copied platform." The framing put BSIQ in a category crowded with similar-sounding claims and invited comparison to Procore, Newmetrix, and other tech platforms — where BSIQ loses on team size, data, and funding.

---

## The New Positioning

> "BuildSafe IQ is a tech-enabled construction risk advisory service — we use AI to score subcontractor and project risk across seven validated signals, then deliver the insight through certified risk professionals so GCs can act before the loss happens."

**AI is the engine. The service is the product.**

The insight: GCs don't buy platforms. Risk managers and CFOs buy outcomes. Certified professionals delivering risk assessments are a known buying category; AI that produces a dashboard is not.

---

## Why This Positioning Wins

**1. Addresses Josh's exact critique.** He built Newmetrix — a platform — and sold it into Oracle where it became enterprise-only. He's telling BSIQ not to repeat the path that ends in an acquisition at the cost of accessibility.

**2. Validated by General Catalyst's Zero RFI thesis.** GC: $13.8M seed, same model: human experts augmented by AI, advisory fees not SaaS subscriptions. Different buyer segment, same economic structure.

**3. Defensible from day one.** A service firm with 5 clients is real revenue. A platform with 5 users is still pre-product-market-fit. The service model generates revenue before the tech is complete — which is the entire revenue-first thesis.

**4. Harder to commoditize.** A SaaS dashboard can be copied. Certified risk professionals with proprietary methodology, embedded in GC workflows, producing insurance outcomes — that's a relationship business with compounding defensibility.

---

## The Revenue-First Model

Full model (under evaluation with Ev):
1. Tech-enabled construction risk consulting + insurance placement generates revenue
2. Revenue funds tech development
3. Tech makes the service scalable (advisors cover more clients with less time)
4. No VC dependency — founder-controlled

Implication: The $2.5M seed raise is under reconsideration. It is not abandoned — but if the service model generates early revenue, the raise timeline and terms change in Aaron's favor.

---

## Trade-offs

| Dimension | SaaS Platform | Tech-Enabled Service |
|---|---|---|
| Investor appeal | Higher (SaaS multiples) | Lower (services multiples) |
| Revenue speed | Slower (build → sell) | Faster (service first, sell as you build) |
| Scalability ceiling | Higher (software margin) | Lower (requires humans) → addressed by tech |
| Defensibility | Lower (copiable) | Higher (relationship + methodology) |
| VC fundability | Yes — standard path | Yes — proven by Zero RFI; non-standard pitch |
| Founder control | Lower (VC dilution pressure) | Higher |
