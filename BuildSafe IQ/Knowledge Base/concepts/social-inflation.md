# Social Inflation — Construction Insurance Impact

Social inflation refers to rising insurance claim costs driven by factors beyond economic inflation: nuclear verdicts, litigation finance, aggressive plaintiff bar tactics, plaintiff-friendly jury pools, and broader definitions of corporate liability. It is the dominant pricing driver across commercial P&C lines in 2025–2026.

---

## What Drives It

- **Nuclear verdicts:** Jury awards exceeding $10M that bear no proportional relationship to actual damages. Increasingly common in bodily injury, product liability, and construction GL.
- **Third-party litigation finance:** Professional investors funding plaintiff lawsuits in exchange for a share of the award. Extends the financial staying power of plaintiffs, forcing larger settlements.
- **Plaintiff-friendly venues:** "Judicial hellholes" (Cook County IL, Broward County FL, Philadelphia PA, NYC) systematically produce higher verdicts.
- **Expanding tort theories:** Courts increasingly holding upstream parties (GCs, owners) responsible for sub-level negligence even with indemnification language.

---

## Construction-Specific Exposure

| Line | Social Inflation Impact |
|---|---|
| **Construction GL — Bodily Injury** | Highest exposure. Steel erection, roofing, and scaffolding injuries produce largest verdicts. Nuclear verdicts on construction sites increasingly common. |
| **Completed Operations** | Long-tail bodily injury claims (e.g., fire from faulty wiring) 10 years post-completion. Litigation finance enables plaintiffs to wait for maximum damages. |
| **Employment Practices Liability** | Wage-and-hour class actions in construction growing. Independent contractor misclassification creates large exposure. |
| **Professional Liability (E&O)** | Design professional claims increasing as buildings get more complex and attribution of defects gets litigated more aggressively. |

## Underwriting Response (Q2 2026)

From Burns & Wilcox Q2 2026 market intel:
- Social inflation cited as dominant theme across all construction lines
- GL premiums for high-severity trades (roofing, steel) continuing upward
- Umbrella/excess market tightening — $10M+ layers becoming more expensive and harder to place
- Carriers requesting more detailed claims history and narrative on social inflation exposure

**BSIQ Application:** Social inflation is a Why Now argument for risk scoring. As GL premiums rise with claim severity, GCs who can demonstrate pre-screened, low-risk subs get better pricing — quantifiable ROI for BSIQ's service.
