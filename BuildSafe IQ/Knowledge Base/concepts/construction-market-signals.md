# Construction Market Signals — Leading Indicators

A framework for reading construction market health at the macro and project level. Useful for identifying risk environments before they materialize into claims or losses.

---

## Macro Market Indicators (Project Oracle Feeds)

**Current Market State (as of May 2026):**
- Construction starts rebounded +13% in March 2026 after soft Q4 2025
- Dodge Momentum Index +2% in March — planning activity rising
- CPI April 2026 showing persistent materials inflation — watch for fixed-price contract distress
- Data center construction surging — $5–15B in uninsured exposure per campus (hyperscale PML modeling gap)
- NYC construction starts rising; DFW multi-sector strength; LA infrastructure program launched

**Key macro indexes to track:**
- Dodge Momentum Index (leading indicator for nonresidential construction, 9–12 month lead time)
- ABC Construction Backlog Indicator (monthly, 6-month rolling average)
- AIA Billings Index (architectural billings → construction starts, 9–12 month lag)
- BLS Construction Employment & CPI — materials component

## Project-Level Delay Signals (Leading Indicators)

From BSIQ's proprietary framework — these signals are observable before project failure occurs:

### Financial Signals
| Signal | Implication |
|---|---|
| Payment delinquencies from GC to sub | Cash constraint at GC level propagating downstream |
| Lien filings on active project | Payment dispute escalating; supply chain at risk |
| Sub's WIP profit fade on this project | Project is running over budget; completion risk rising |
| Sub pulling workers to another job | Prioritizing more profitable work; schedule impact imminent |

### Labor Signals
| Signal | Implication |
|---|---|
| Turnover spike on key crew | Experienced workers leaving; productivity and quality at risk |
| Foreman replacement mid-project | Disruption to field management; change order risk rises |
| Crew reduction without completion milestone | Scope reduction or cash constraint |

### Schedule Signals
| Signal | Implication |
|---|---|
| RFI volume spike | Design gaps being exposed; every unanswered RFI is a potential delay claim |
| Milestone slippage compounding | Recovery plans rarely work; schedule overrun is becoming permanent |
| Punchlist growing without resolution | Quality management failure; completion and retainage risk |

### Procurement Signals
| Signal | Implication |
|---|---|
| Lead time extensions on long-lead items | Supply chain disruption; critical path at risk |
| Material substitution requests mid-project | Originally specified materials unavailable or unaffordable |
| Supplier credit holds | Sub is not paying suppliers; supply chain will freeze |

### Behavioral Signals
| Signal | Implication |
|---|---|
| Increase in formal written correspondence vs. informal | Parties building a legal record — dispute escalation |
| Change order disputes without resolution | Scope disagreement; litigation risk rising |
| Executive involvement in project-level issues | Problem has exceeded PM authority |

## Surety Early Warning Sequence

From Southland Holdings (2026) and historical pattern: rapid revenue growth → WIP profit fade → overbillings → cash depletion → surety activation. BSIQ risk scoring catches this at Step 1–2.

## Social Inflation Signal

Social inflation (rising claim severity from litigation finance, nuclear verdicts, plaintiff-friendly venues) is the dominant theme across all P&C lines in 2026. Construction is particularly exposed through: GL bodily injury on high-severity trades (steel, roofing), employment practices (wage-and-hour class actions), and completed operations long-tail claims.
