# Subcontractor Prequalification

The structured process by which a GC evaluates whether a subcontractor is qualified to perform a specific scope of work before awarding a contract. The goal is predicting probability of default — not confirming the sub can do the work, but confirming they can do this work, on this project, at this size, right now.

Per CFMA: *"Subcontractor prequalification criteria are akin to surety bond underwriting."*

---

## The 3 Cs Framework

| C | Core Question | Primary Data Sources |
|---|---|---|
| **Capital** | Can they absorb project setbacks without defaulting? | Financial statements, WIP schedule, AR/AP aging, bank letter, bonding capacity |
| **Capacity** | Do they have the workforce, equipment, and experience for this specific scope? | Project history, headcount, equipment schedule, QMP, references |
| **Character** | Have they behaved reliably, safely, and ethically — and will they continue to? | Safety records, litigation history, payment practices, regulatory compliance |

---

## Financial Documentation Requirements by Contract Value

| Contract Value | Required Statement Level | Supporting Documents |
|---|---|---|
| Under $500K | Company-prepared | Basic financials |
| $500K – $2M | CPA-reviewed (SSARS) | WIP, AR/AP aging, bank letter |
| $2M – $5M | CPA-reviewed or audited | All above + interim financials (within 90 days) |
| Over $5M | Fully audited (GAAS) | All above + bonding capacity letter, credit report |

## Universal Automatic Disqualifiers

These override all positive signals in any trade:

| Condition | Threshold |
|---|---|
| EMR exceeds hard cutoff | > 1.25 most programs; > 1.40 roofing/framing |
| TRIR exceeds cutoff | > 4.0 general; trade-adjusted per [[safety-metrics]] |
| Current Ratio below floor | < 1.1 |
| Bankruptcy in past 7 years | Any filing |
| Active termination for cause | Any |
| Surety won't bond proposed project | Any — most powerful negative signal |
| Trade license lapsed or suspended | Any |
| GL carrier below A- / AM Best | Any |
| Net Profit Margin below 2% | Razor-thin = job borrowing risk |
| Days of Cash on Hand below 10 days | Cannot sustain operations |
| Fatality in past 3 years with low TRIR | Suggests underreporting |

---

## The Surety Bond as Ultimate Prequal Tool

A T-List surety that issues a performance and payment bond has already run the most rigorous prequal process available. **If a surety will not provide a bondability letter for the proposed project — this is the single most powerful negative signal in the entire framework.** It overrides positive financials, clean safety records, and strong references.

Bondability letter must confirm both single-project limit and aggregate capacity, from a Treasury-listed surety, dated within 90 days.

---

## ROI of Prequalification (for GC)

- Reduces WC claims piercing GC deductible
- Reduces GL claims from sub negligence
- Reduces SDI (Subcontractor Default Insurance) deductible triggers
- Improves GC's own EMR over time → lower WC premiums
- Strengthens GC surety relationship
