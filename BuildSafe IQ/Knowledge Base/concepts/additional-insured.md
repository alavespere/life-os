# Additional Insured Endorsements

The mechanism by which a GC (or owner) extends the protection of a subcontractor's CGL policy to itself. It is the primary contractual risk transfer tool in construction and the most commonly defective element of a subcontractor's insurance program.

---

## The Two Required Endorsements

Both are required. One alone leaves a gap.

| Endorsement | ISO Form | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| **CG 20 10** | Additional Insured — Owners, Lessees or Contractors (Ongoing Operations) | Liability arising from the sub's ongoing work on the project |
| **CG 20 37** | Additional Insured — Owners, Lessees or Contractors (Completed Operations) | Liability arising after the work is complete — the long-tail exposure |

A COI that shows "Additional Insured" without specifying CG 20 10 + CG 20 37 is insufficient. Require copies of the actual endorsements.

---

## Primary and Non-Contributory Language

Without this endorsement, a sub's CGL and a GC's own CGL are typically triggered proportionally when both are named on a claim. P&NC language requires the sub's policy to pay first and in full before the GC's own policy contributes — protecting the GC's aggregate and deductible.

**How to verify:** The COI will often state "Primary and Non-Contributory" but the actual endorsement must confirm. Common form: CG 20 01 or equivalent carrier endorsement.

---

## Waiver of Subrogation

Prevents the sub's insurer from suing the GC to recover claim payments after paying a loss. Without this, a sub's carrier can pay a claim and then pursue the GC for the portion of fault it can attribute to the GC's negligence.

Required on: CGL, Workers' Compensation, and Commercial Auto.

---

## Recent Case Law Signal

Fourth Circuit (2026): Extended additional insured coverage to contractor under circumstances where AI language was broadly written. Direction in case law is toward broader AI enforcement when contract language supports it — but the endorsement form must be correct. COI shortcuts and blanket AI language without the actual endorsement forms remain high-risk for GCs.

---

## Common AI Defects Found in Practice

| Defect | Risk to GC |
|---|---|
| CG 20 10 present but CG 20 37 absent | GC exposed for completed operations — the long-tail risk |
| AI language on COI only, no endorsement copy | Certificate ≠ policy; carrier may deny coverage |
| AI endorsement limits coverage to "sole negligence" | Significantly narrows coverage; reject and require broad form |
| Policy not primary and non-contributory | GC's own policy contributes before sub's exhausts |
| Umbrella doesn't follow form | Gap between primary and umbrella on AI coverage |
