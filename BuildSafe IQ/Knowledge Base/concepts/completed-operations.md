# Completed Operations — The Coverage Gap

The most common uncovered liability in commercial construction. Standard CGL policies extend completed operations coverage for 2 years post-completion. Commercial roofing warranties run 10–20 years. Fire sprinkler and HVAC failures can manifest 5–10 years later. The gap between "what the policy covers" and "when the loss actually happens" is where GCs absorb uninsured losses from their subs' work.

---

## The Gap by Trade

| Trade | Standard CGL Completed Ops | Required Minimum | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Roofing | 2 years | **5+ years; 10 years preferred** | Match to manufacturer warranty period — Firestone, GAF run 20-year NDL warranties |
| Fire Sprinkler | 2 years | **5+ years** | System failure triggers building-wide loss; single incident can exhaust sub's aggregate |
| Steel/Precast | 2 years | 5 years recommended | Structural defects are inherently long-tail |
| HVAC/Plumbing | 2 years | 3–5 years recommended | Water damage manifests years later |
| Electrical | 2 years | 3 years recommended | Wiring failures often manifest 2–5 years post-completion |
| Masonry | 2 years | 3 years recommended | Spalling, water infiltration are long-tail |

---

## Why This Gap Is Routinely Missed

1. COI review focuses on limits, not completed ops extension
2. GC contracts specify $1M/$2M limits but don't require extended completed ops endorsements
3. Roofing subs often use E&S carriers (harder to verify endorsement language)
4. Sub's "occurrence" policy appears correct but completed ops sunset creates an effective claims-made policy

---

## How to Close the Gap

1. Require extended completed ops endorsements in subcontract — specifying minimum years
2. Verify via endorsement copy (not COI alone)
3. For roofing and fire sprinkler, match extension period to the warranty term being issued on the project
4. Confirm umbrella/excess follows form for completed ops — many umbrella policies specifically carve out completed operations

---

## Red Flags

- Roofing sub with completed ops limited to 2 years on a project with a 20-year NDL warranty
- COI states "completed operations included" without specifying the extension period
- Fire sprinkler sub using claims-made form (completed ops on claims-made = no coverage once policy lapses)
- Policy non-renewed after project completion — all completed ops coverage disappears with the policy
