# Commercial General Liability (CGL) — Construction Context

CGL is the foundational liability policy for every contractor. In construction, CGL is complicated by long-tail exposures (damage that manifests years after work is complete), multi-party liability chains (owner → GC → sub → sub-sub), and the completed operations gap that standard policies routinely undercover.

---

## Standard CGL Minimums for Subcontractors

| Coverage | Minimum Limits | Critical Endorsements Required |
|---|---|---|
| CGL | $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate | CG 20 10 (AI — Ongoing Ops) + CG 20 37 (AI — Completed Ops); Primary & Non-Contributory; Waiver of Subrogation |
| Workers' Compensation | Statutory per state | Waiver of Subrogation in favor of GC |
| Employers' Liability | $1M / $1M / $1M | — |
| Commercial Auto | $1M CSL | AI endorsement |
| Umbrella/Excess | $5M–$25M (project-dependent) | Follow-form over all underlying |

---

## The Completed Operations Problem

The most common uncovered liability in construction. Commercial roofing warranties run 10–20 years. Most standard CGL policies only extend completed operations coverage for 2 years after project completion. This creates a gap where the GC is exposed for the sub's work with no insurance behind it.

| Trade | Standard Completed Ops | Required Minimum |
|---|---|---|
| Roofing | 2 years | **5+ years; 10 preferred** — match to manufacturer warranty |
| Fire Sprinkler | 2 years | **5+ years** |
| Steel/Precast | 2 years | 5 years recommended |
| HVAC/Plumbing | 2 years | 3–5 years recommended |
| Electrical | 2 years | 3 years recommended |

## E&S Market — When to Expect It

Some trades routinely land in Excess & Surplus (non-admitted) markets. E&S is not disqualifying — but verify (1) carrier AM Best A- minimum, (2) no exclusions that leave GC exposed, (3) occurrence basis preferred over claims-made for long-tail trades.

Trades likely to be E&S: commercial roofing (torch-applied, BUR), steel erection (high-rise), blasting/demolition, bridge/industrial painting, asbestos/lead abatement.

---

## COI Verification Checklist

A certificate of insurance ≠ a policy. Always require actual endorsements for high-risk trades — COIs can reflect coverage that doesn't actually exist or has exclusions not visible on the face of the cert.

- [ ] No gaps in policy dates
- [ ] Carrier AM Best minimum **A- / VII**
- [ ] CG 20 10 (ongoing ops AI) + CG 20 37 (completed ops AI) confirmed
- [ ] Primary and non-contributory language confirmed
- [ ] Waiver of subrogation on all lines
- [ ] Limits adequate for proposed contract value
- [ ] Completed ops extension adequate for trade (see table above)

## Key Holland Book Principles (CGL in Construction)

From J. Kent Holland Jr., *Construction Law & Risk Management*:

- **Additional Insured endorsements** are specifically construed — CG 20 10 covers ongoing operations, CG 20 37 covers completed operations. Both are required; one alone leaves a gap.
- **Design-build contractors** cannot rely on standard CGL for design liability — E&O is required. CGL specifically excludes professional services.
- **Environmental exposures** (CERCLA, mold, contamination) are excluded from standard CGL — CPL is required.
- **"Continuous and progressive damage"** exclusions can eliminate CGL coverage for damage that occurs gradually (water intrusion, mold, corrosion) — policy form matters.
