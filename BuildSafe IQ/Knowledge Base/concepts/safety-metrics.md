# Safety Metrics — EMR, TRIR, DART by Trade

Sources: BLS Table 1 (2024), ABC STEP (2025), NCCI, CFMA 2024. Safety metrics are the most visible and commonly misinterpreted dimension of subcontractor risk. EMR is a relative measure — it must be read against the trade average, not against 1.0 universally.

---

## Definitions

| Metric | What It Measures | How to Calculate |
|---|---|---|
| **EMR** (Experience Modification Rate) | WC claim history vs. expected for trade and size. 1.0 = industry average. | Calculated by NCCI/state bureau from 3 years of WC payroll and losses |
| **TRIR** (Total Recordable Incident Rate) | All OSHA-recordable injuries per 100 FTE per year | (Number of OSHA recordables × 200,000) / Total hours worked |
| **DART** (Days Away, Restricted, or Transferred) | Injuries serious enough to restrict work | Same formula, restricted to DART cases only |
| **DAFW** (Days Away From Work) | Subset of DART — only cases with days away | Subset of DART formula |

## Safety Red Flags (All Trades)

| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| EMR trending upward over 3 years | Worsening safety culture — lagging indicator |
| OSHA willful violations in past 5 years | Deliberate disregard for worker safety |
| Fatality in past 3 years | Investigate circumstances and corrective action |
| TRIR well below trade average but DART is high | Few incidents but severe; or underreporting of minor injuries |
| **Fatality + low TRIR** | Strongest underreporting signal — investigate immediately |
| TRIR below 1.5 on commercial roofing | Well-documented underreporting; scrutinize |
| No designated Safety Officer | Safety is not a management priority |
| OSHA 300 logs not signed by executive | Non-compliance with recordkeeping |
| Drug and alcohol testing policy absent | High risk for heavy equipment trades |
