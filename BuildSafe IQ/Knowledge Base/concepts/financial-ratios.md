# Financial Ratios — Construction Subcontractor Benchmarks

Source: CFMA 2024 Financial Benchmarker (specialty trade contractors). Use these to score subcontractor financials — not to declare a sub healthy or unhealthy in isolation, but to flag anomalies that warrant deeper investigation.

**The most important document in subcontractor financial prequal is the WIP schedule — not the balance sheet.** Standard financials are backward-looking; WIP reveals real-time project health.

---

## Liquidity Ratios

| Metric | Formula | CFMA Avg | Best in Class | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | ~1.6 | > 2.0 | < 1.1 |
| Quick Ratio | (Cash + Securities + AR) / Current Liabilities | ~1.4 | > 1.8 | < 1.0 |
| Days of Cash on Hand | Cash / ((Operating Exp – Depreciation) / 365) | ~22 days | > 40 days | < 10 days |
| Underbillings to Working Capital | Underbillings / Working Capital | ~8% | < 5% | > 25% |

> Days of Cash ~22 days is the industry average. Awarding a large contract to a firm with <10 days of cash means the GC is effectively providing an unsecured line of credit.

---

## Profitability Ratios

| Metric | Formula | CFMA Avg | Best in Class | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Gross Profit Margin | (Revenue – Cost of Sales) / Revenue | ~15–16% | 21.8% | < 10% |
| Net Profit Margin (Pre-Tax) | Net Income Before Tax / Revenue | 6.9% | 11.9% | < 2% |
| Return on Assets (ROA) | Net Income / Total Assets | 13.4% | 28.4% | < 5% |
| Revenue per FTE | Total Revenue / FTEs | $450K | — | — |

> Net Margin below 2% = operating on razor-thin margins. High risk of **job borrowing** — using cash from profitable jobs to fund losses elsewhere. #1 precursor to subcontractor default.

---

## Leverage Ratios

| Metric | Formula | CFMA Avg | Best in Class | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Debt to Equity | Total Liabilities / Total Equity | 1.3x | 0.9x | > 3.0x |
| Times Interest Earned | EBIT / Interest Expense | 25.6x | — | < 5.0x |
| Fixed Asset Ratio | Fixed Assets / Total Assets | 23.7% | 12.9% | > 50% |
| Revenue to Equity | Total Revenue / Total Equity | 5.4x | — | > 15x |

---

## Efficiency & Backlog Ratios

| Metric | Formula | CFMA Avg | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Days in AR | (AR / Revenue) × 365 | 56.6 days | > 75 days |
| Days in AP | (AP / Revenue) × 365 | 33.0 days | AP >> AR = distress signal |
| Months in Backlog | (Backlog / Annual Revenue) × 12 | 8.7 months | < 3 or > 18 months |
| Backlog to Working Capital | Backlog / Working Capital | 5.9x | > 10x |

---

## WIP Schedule Analysis

The WIP schedule reveals what balance sheets hide: are active projects gaining or losing margin in real time?

**Critical WIP Metrics:**

| Metric | Red Flag Threshold | What It Signals |
|---|---|---|
| **Profit Fade** | Consistent fade on 2+ active projects | #1 predictor of financial distress. Poor estimating or field cost control failure. |
| Underbillings to Working Capital | > 15% | Unapproved change orders, billing delays, or disguised project losses |
| Cash to Overbillings Ratio | < 0.50 | Overbilled cash already spent — job borrowing |
| Backlog to Working Capital | > 10x | Attempting to execute far more than financial resources support |

**Profit Fade calculation:** (Original GP% at bid) – (Current estimated GP% on WIP) across all active jobs. Any consistent fade across 2+ projects = investigate immediately.

---

## Financial Red Flags Summary

**Balance Sheet:**
- AR Days > 75: slow collections; may indicate disputes or working with slow-paying GCs
- AP Days significantly > 33: distress — suppliers will pull credit
- Debt-to-Equity > 3.0x: vulnerable to any cost overrun or payment delay
- Revenue-to-Equity > 15x: collapse risk if one project performs poorly
- Fixed Asset Ratio > 50%: illiquid balance sheet

**Revenue:**
- Growth > 30% YoY without headcount growth: scaling faster than management can handle
- Decline > 20% YoY: loss of key clients or market position — investigate before awarding
- Single GC > 40% of backlog: dependency risk
