# Trade Benchmarks Master

**Source:** BLS Table 1 (2024), ABC STEP (2025), NCCI, CFMA 2024 Financial Benchmarker, LumberFi EMR Guide (2025)
**Processed:** 2026-05-12
**Purpose:** Input data for Risk Scoring Model, Subcontractor Prequal Module, Underwriting Tool

---

## What This Source Contains

The authoritative, structured dataset for evaluating any subcontractor against trade-calibrated benchmarks. Seven data dimensions across 14 specialty trade types.

| Part | Content |
|---|---|
| Part I | Safety benchmarks by trade — TRIR, DART, DAFW, EMR averages and thresholds |
| Part II | Workers' Compensation profiles — NCCI codes, loss costs per $100 payroll |
| Part III | Trade-calibrated EMR and TRIR scoring tables (1–5 scale) |
| Part IV | Financial benchmarks by trade — margins, current ratio, AR days, revenue/employee |
| Part V | Surety bonding profiles — single project and aggregate limits |
| Part VI | GL risk tiers by trade — monthly GL costs, primary loss drivers |
| Part VII | NAICS code quick reference |

## Content Engine Applications (from source)

Per the source's "Part V — Content Engine" note, counterintuitive data points for content:

- Steel erection WC costs 11x more per $100 payroll than electrical — most GCs have no idea
- Framing has higher TRIR than roofing (4.6 vs 2.4) — counterintuitive given roofing's fatal fall reputation
- Average specialty trade sub has only 22 days of cash on hand — GCs are effectively unsecured lenders
- Net margin below 2% (red flag) is only one percentage point from the industry average of 6.9% — the margin for error is thin
