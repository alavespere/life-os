# Format: Long-Form Article
_Read this file before generating any long-form content._

---

## Reference
→ Voice & Style Guide.md (read first)

## Structure
```
[HEADLINE — states the thesis or the anomaly. Not a question. Not a listicle title.]

[OPENING — 1–2 paragraphs. Hook + the problem. Thesis stated by paragraph 2.]

[SECTION 1 — The Evidence]
Why this problem is real. Data, case, or industry pattern.

[SECTION 2 — The Mechanism]
How it works. Why it exists. What most people get wrong about it.

[SECTION 3 — The Turn]
The non-obvious truth. What changes when you see it clearly.

[SECTION 4 — The Implication]
What GCs, subs, or developers should do differently.

[LANDING — One sentence. The whole piece in a single idea. No "in conclusion."]
```

## Specs
- Length: 800–2,000 words (target 1,200)
- Thesis: stated by paragraph 2, not revealed at the end
- Every section must advance the argument — no filler
- Use real numbers, real project types, real scenarios (anonymize if needed)
- Subheadings are functional, not decorative — they tell you what the section proves

## Hard Rules
- No "In today's construction industry..."
- No summary section at the end
- No weak landing ("work with the right partners")
- Specific > general at every opportunity
