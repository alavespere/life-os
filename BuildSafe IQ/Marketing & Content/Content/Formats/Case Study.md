# Format: Case Study
_Read this file before generating any case study content._

---

## Reference
→ Voice & Style Guide.md (read first)

## Structure
```
[HEADLINE — lead with the outcome, not the setup]
Example: "A GC walked away from a $2.1M claim with $0 out of pocket. Here's what they did differently."

[THE SITUATION — 1 paragraph]
Who, what project type, what exposure. Specific. No vague "a mid-size contractor."

[THE RISK SIGNAL — 1–2 paragraphs]
What was the warning sign. What data or pattern was present before the event.
This is the section that makes readers uncomfortable — they recognize their own situation.

[THE DECISION — 1 paragraph]
What they did. Specific action, not general principle.

[THE OUTCOME — 1 paragraph]
What happened. Numbers when possible. Real consequences.

[THE LESSON — 1–2 paragraphs]
The extractable insight. Must be actionable. Not "work with a good broker."
What specifically can a GC or sub do Monday morning.

[LANDING — one sentence. The thesis of the whole piece.]
```

## Specs
- Anonymize client names unless permission given — keep all numbers real
- Lead with outcome in headline and opening — never bury it
- The risk signal section is the most important — this is where BuildSafe IQ's value lives
- Lesson must be specific and actionable

## Hard Rules
- Never lead with company background
- Never end with a soft CTA ("reach out to learn more")
- If the numbers aren't specific, the case study isn't ready to publish
