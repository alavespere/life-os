## Two Modes

### Short (150–300 words)
```
[HOOK — one line, no warmup, creates tension or drops a hard fact]

[2–3 lines of context — what's broken, what most people miss]

[The evidence — one specific detail, number, or case that makes it real]

[The turn — the non-obvious truth or reframe]

[Hard landing — one sentence. The whole point. No CTA.]
```

### Long (400–700 words)
```
[HOOK — same rule]

[The problem — 2–3 short paragraphs, each earning the next]

[The evidence — specific, grounded, real]

[The turn — where the piece goes from interesting to useful]

[The landing — sharp. Final. One idea.]
```

## Hard Rules
- First line must work without the rest of the post — it's the only thing most people read
- Line breaks every 2–3 lines maximum
- No "thoughts?" or "drop a comment below"
- No "I'm excited to share"
- No listicles as the primary structure
- Maximum 1 emoji — only if it adds signal
- One idea per post. Not five.
