## The Johnny Harris Method (applied to construction risk)

Johnny Harris doesn't explain topics. He **investigates anomalies.**

The question is never "what is construction risk insurance?"
The question is "why do 70% of GCs have the wrong coverage — and nobody's talking about it?"

Every video starts from a place of **genuine curiosity about something that doesn't add up.**

---

## Script Structure

```
[COLD OPEN — 15–30 seconds]
Visual scene or hard fact. No introduction. No "hey guys."
Drop the viewer into the middle of something real.
Voice is calm, slightly urgent. Like you're about to show them something.

[THE QUESTION — 30–60 seconds]
State the anomaly or the thing that doesn't make sense.
"Here's what I couldn't figure out..."
"This number doesn't add up — and when I looked into it, what I found was..."
Build tension. Don't answer it yet.

[THE EVIDENCE — 2–4 minutes]
Build the case. Data, examples, visuals, real scenarios.
Each piece of evidence tightens the argument.
Pacing: SHORT sentences at impact beats. Longer sentences for context.
Pivots: "But here's what nobody talks about." / "Here's the thing." (use sparingly)

[THE TURN — 60–90 seconds]
The non-obvious truth. What you now understand that most people don't.
This is the payoff for the tension you built.

[THE LANDING — 30–45 seconds]
One idea. The thesis, fully landed.
No channel plug in the middle of the landing.
End on the idea — not on housekeeping.
```

---

## Pacing Notes

- Short sentences = emphasis and drive
- Long sentences = context, complexity, building toward something
- Never two long sentences back to back without a short one
- Silence (in video) = emphasis — write pauses into the script with `[pause]`

---

## Voice Notes for Scripts
- First person, singular — "I found", "I couldn't figure out", "what I learned was"
- Speak to one person — not "you all" or "folks"
- Never announce what you're about to do — just do it
- No "in this video I'll be covering..."

---

## Hard Rules
- Every script has ONE thesis. One. Build the entire video to prove it.
- Cold open has no warm-up — viewer is in by second 5 or they're gone
- Never end on "let me know in the comments" as the final thought
- Scripts are written to be spoken — read aloud during drafting, cut anything that doesn't sound like Aaron talking
