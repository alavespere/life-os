### Risk Was Predictable
- **"Why Construction Contracts Are Still Stuck in 1985"**
  - ISO CGL forms haven't been updated since 1985–86. Attorneys still reference terms like "comprehensive general liability" and "broad form property damage" that no longer exist.
  - Hook: "Your construction contract is referencing an insurance product that was discontinued before the internet existed."

- **"The Safety Meeting That Actually Increases Risk"**
  - Compliance theater creates false security while real risks develop undetected
  - Talking about safety ≠ being safe. Companies spend millions of hours discussing what could go wrong while investing virtually nothing in systems that identify what *is* going wrong.

- **"The $42M Dispute No One Saw Coming"**
  - Average construction dispute: 14–15 months, $42.8M per case
  - These weren't surprises. The signals were there.

### Risk Is Mispriced
- **"Why Lowest Bid Procurement Is Killing Construction Projects"**
  - Contractors bid low, recoup through change orders. Schedule pressure prevents scrutiny. The client pays twice.
  - Lowest bid = lowest price. Not lowest cost.

- **"The Hidden Cost of Your Group Captive"**
  - Heterogeneous captives penalize well-managed firms. Your rate reflects the worst member, not your actual risk profile.

- **"How Your Insurance Broker Is Accidentally Sabotaging Your Profit Margin"**
  - Brokers optimize for placement, not TCOR. The incentive structure is broken.

### Data Changes Everything
- **"The Data That Predicts Subcontractor Default — 6 Months Out"**
  - Billing vs. progress mismatch. Capacity overextension signals. All publicly available or platform-derived.
  - Reference: Bryan Builders $19M case study

- **"Construction's Dirty Secret: Everyone Knows Projects Will Fail"**
  - 91.5% fail to finish on time or on budget (How Big Things Get Done, 16,000 projects)
  - The data is decades old. The pattern is unchanged. Why?

- **"The Permit Data No One Is Reading"**
  - Census Bureau, local building departments, inspection records — all public, all predictive
  - Most GCs have never looked at it.

### Insurance System Is Broken
- **"Why Construction Attorneys Don't Understand Insurance"**
  - Attorneys draft contracts but aren't licensed in insurance and aren't required to keep current on evolving forms
  - Result: contracts create coverage gaps before projects even start

- **"Toxic Contracts: Built on Vinegar, Not Honey"**
  - American construction contracts are combative and punitive, not collaborative
  - The contract sets the tone for the entire project relationship

- **"Why Are Florida Projects Going Uninsured?"**
  - High-risk markets pricing out coverage. The cascade effect.

## Trade Intelligence — Subcontractor Risk (High Priority, Data-Rich)
_Source: BuildSafe IQ Research — Subcontractor Prequalification Benchmarks by Trade Vertical (2026-05-09)_
_All data points are cited and verifiable. Pillar: The Human Factor + Risk Was Predictable._

- **"The Most Dangerous Trade in Construction Isn't Roofing"**
  - Framing has a TRIR of 4.6 — 100% above the specialty trade average. Roofing gets all the attention. Framing kills more workers per hour worked.
  - Hook: "Everyone's afraid of roofers. Nobody's talking about framers."

- **"Your Roofing Sub's Safety Record Is Probably Fake"**
  - Roofing has a documented underreporting problem. A TRIR below 1.5 is a red flag, not a green one. The fatality rate tells a different story than the TRIR.
  - Data: 2.56 fatalities per 100,000 workers; industry average EMR exceeds 1.0

- **"Steel Erection WC Costs 11x More Than Electrical. Most GCs Don't Know That."**
  - NCCI code 5069 (~$60.61/100 payroll) vs. 5190 (~$5.49/100). The difference is material to project cost and subcontractor financial health.
  - Angle: If your steel sub is undercapitalized, the WC premium alone can bankrupt them mid-project.

- **"Siding Contractors Are More Dangerous Than Roofers — by One Measure"**
  - Siding DART rate: 2.5. Roofing DART rate: 1.7. Nobody knows this. DART captures severity, not just frequency.
  - Hook: "The trade that's putting workers in the hospital at the highest rate isn't the one you're watching."

- **"The Roofing Sub with a 1.5 TRIR Should Scare You More Than the One with a 3.0"**
  - Counterintuitive: a roofing sub with a very low TRIR has a high probability of underreporting. The industry average EMR exceeds 1.0 — a 1.0 EMR roofer is actually performing above average.

- **"Water Is Construction's Most Expensive Claim. Here's Who's Causing It."**
  - HVAC/Plumbing is the highest GL risk cluster for water damage. Fire sprinkler is even higher. Most GCs don't adjust GL requirements by trade.
  - Data: Water damage = most common and expensive GL claim in construction

- **"The Certificate of Insurance Is a Lie"**
  - A COI shows coverage exists. It doesn't show trade-specific exclusions, completed ops sunset clauses, shared aggregates, or whether the E&S carrier can actually pay the claim.
  - Pillar: The Insurance Maze

- **"Why Your Roofing Sub Needs 10-Year Completed Operations Coverage"**
  - Standard CGL: 2 years. Commercial roofing warranty: 10–20 years. The gap is your problem.
  - Specific, actionable, legally important.

- **"The WIP Schedule Predicts Subcontractor Default 6 Months Out"**
  - Profit fade across multiple active projects is the #1 predictor of financial distress. The data is on the WIP. Most GCs never ask for it.
  - Connect to existing backlog idea: "The Data That Predicts Subcontractor Default — 6 Months Out"

- **"Days of Cash: The Number That Tells You If Your Sub Can Survive This Project"**
  - CFMA average: 23.5 days of cash. A sub with <10 days of cash cannot sustain operations through a payment delay. You're funding them.
  - Simple, scary, understandable by any GC.

- **"The Lowest Bid Electrical Sub Has 11x Less Workers' Comp Exposure Than Your Steel Sub — and Nobody Accounts for That"**
  - WC cost differential by trade is a hidden project cost that most GCs don't model. It affects a sub's working capital and ability to sustain operations.

- **"What the Surety Knows That You Don't"**
  - If a surety won't write a bond for a sub on your project, that's the most rigorous negative prequalification signal available. The surety has seen the financial statements. Listen to them.
  - Pillar: The Contract Battlefield

- **"Job Borrowing: The Quiet Way Subcontractors Fail"**
  - Using cash from profitable projects to fund losses on others. It shows up as low Cash-to-Overbillings ratios and underbillings on the WIP. By the time it shows up on the P&L, it's too late.

- **"Silica Is the Asbestos of This Decade — And Your Masonry Sub Is Probably Exposed"**
  - OSHA 1926.1153 silica standard is already law. Most masonry and tile subs don't have compliant programs. The GC is liable for what happens on their site.

- **"Why Framing Contractors Have Thinner Margins Than Any Other Trade — and What That Means When Lumber Prices Spike"**
  - Framing: 15–22% gross margin, 2–4% net. Lumber price volatility of 50–100% wipes that out entirely. A fixed-price framing contract during a spike is a default waiting to happen.
