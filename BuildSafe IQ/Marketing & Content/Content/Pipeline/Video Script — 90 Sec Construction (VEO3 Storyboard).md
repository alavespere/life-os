## Clip 1: "The Illusion of Control" (0:00–0:08)

**Scene:** Large construction site in semi-chaos — materials stacked incorrectly, a backhoe reversing, someone on a ladder yelling instructions. Executive walks onto site.

**Characters:**
- Executive: Male, mid-50s, clean-cut, khakis and button-down, clipboard. Slightly skeptical.
- PM: Mid-30s, reflective vest, hard hat, forced calmness masking stress.

**Dialogue:**
- Executive (calm, concerned): "So, how's the project coming along?"
- PM (forced smile): "Great! We're only behind schedule and over budget!"

**Camera:** Medium wide from behind executive → cut to close-up of PM's forced smile → subtle zoom on PM's face.

---

## Clip 2: "Silos & Stagnation" (0:08–0:16)

**Scene:** Job trailer packed with tech — laptops, whiteboards, dual monitors. Three people sitting next to each other, working in completely different systems.

**Characters:**
- Superintendent: Mid-40s, rugged, dirty vest. On the phone, yelling about a delivery.
- Estimator: 30s, clean, button-down. Staring confused at an Excel sheet.
- Engineer: Late 20s, hoodie + hard hat, toggling between four apps.

**Voiceover:** "We've got more tools than ever — but the processes? Still stuck in 1985."

**Background dialogue:**
- Superintendent: "Where's that delivery? That was supposed to be here yesterday!"
- Estimator: "That's not in my spreadsheet."
- Engineer: (shrugs at RFI status)

---

## Production Notes
- Style: Dry, slightly absurdist. The humor comes from recognition, not exaggeration.
- Tone: Like a documentary that's been on a job site — not a corporate explainer video.
- Each clip ends on a visual beat, not a line of dialogue.
- The overall arc: chaos is normal → chaos is not inevitable → BuildSafe IQ changes the equation.
