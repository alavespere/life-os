## Part I: Standard Required Coverages (All Trades)

Every subcontractor, regardless of trade, must carry the following minimum coverages:

| Coverage | Minimum Limits | Critical Endorsements |
|---|---|---|
| **Commercial General Liability (CGL)** | $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate | CG 20 10 (Additional Insured — Ongoing Ops) + CG 20 37 (Additional Insured — Completed Ops); Primary & Non-Contributory; Waiver of Subrogation |
| **Workers' Compensation** | Statutory limits per state | Waiver of Subrogation in favor of GC |
| **Employers' Liability** | $1M / $1M / $1M | — |
| **Commercial Auto Liability** | $1M combined single limit | Additional Insured endorsement |
| **Excess / Umbrella** | $5M–$25M (project-dependent) | Follow-form over all underlying policies |

**Certificate of Insurance Verification Checklist:**
- [ ] No gaps in policy dates
- [ ] Carrier A.M. Best rating minimum **A- / VII**
- [ ] Correct additional insured language (CG 20 10 + CG 20 37)
- [ ] Primary and non-contributory language confirmed
- [ ] Waiver of subrogation on all lines
- [ ] Limits adequate for proposed contract value

## Part II: Specialty Coverage Requirements by Trade

| Coverage Type | When Required | Trades |
|---|---|---|
| **Contractors Pollution Liability (CPL)** | Handling refrigerants, chemicals, near waterways, excavation/underground work, lead/solvent paint, hazardous locations | HVAC (refrigerants), Site Prep (all), Painting (lead/solvent), Electrical (hazardous locations), Concrete (near waterways) |
| **Professional Liability (E&O)** | Design-build or design-assist scope | Electrical (design-build), HVAC/Mechanical (mechanical design), Concrete (if design), Site Prep (civil design) |
| **Installation Floater** | Equipment/materials in transit or during installation | Steel/Precast (curtain wall, large glazing panels), Glass & Glazing |
| **Riggers Liability** | Crane operations, heavy lifts | Steel Erection (**required**), Concrete (large pours with crane), HVAC (large equipment lifts) |
| **Hot Work Endorsement** | Torch-applied systems, welding, soldering | HVAC/Plumbing (soldering), Roofing (torch-applied membranes), Steel/Welding, Painting (torch work) |
| **Arc Flash / Electrical Hazard Endorsement** | Electrical work — especially panels, switchgear | Electrical (recommended, increasingly required) |
| **Silica Liability Endorsement** | Crystalline silica exposure under OSHA 1926.1153 | Masonry (**required**), Concrete (recommended), Tile/Terrazzo, Drywall/Insulation (cutting/sanding) |
| **Extended Completed Operations (5+ years)** | Long-tail exposure — leaks, structural, fire | Roofing (**required** — warranties 10–20 years), Fire Sprinkler (**required**), Steel, Electrical, HVAC |

## Part IV: GL Minimum Limits by Trade Risk Tier

| GL Risk Tier | Trades | Standard Minimum Limits | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Extreme** | Roofing, Steel Erection | $2M per occurrence / $4M aggregate + $5M–$10M umbrella | Non-admitted carrier acceptable only if A- rated by AM Best; require declarations page |
| **Very High** | Fire Sprinkler, HVAC/Plumbing (water risk) | $2M / $4M recommended | Extended completed ops required |
| **High** | Concrete, Masonry, Electrical | $1M / $2M minimum; $2M / $4M preferred for large projects | Verify completed ops is not sunset |
| **Moderate** | Drywall, Painting, Site Prep, Flooring | $1M / $2M standard | CPL required for site prep and certain painting scopes |

## Part V: Completed Operations — Extension Requirements by Trade

Commercial roofing warranties run 10–20 years. Most standard CGL policies only extend completed ops for 2 years. This gap is the most common uncovered liability in construction.

| Trade | Standard CGL Completed Ops | Required Minimum | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Roofing | 2 years | **5+ years; 10 preferred** | Match to manufacturer warranty period |
| Fire Sprinkler | 2 years | **5+ years** | System failure can trigger building-wide loss |
| Steel/Precast | 2 years | 5 years recommended | Structural defects are long-tail |
| HVAC/Plumbing | 2 years | 3–5 years recommended | Water damage is long-tail |
| Electrical | 2 years | 3 years recommended | Wiring failures can manifest years later |
| Masonry | 2 years | 3 years recommended | Spalling, water infiltration |

## Part VI: E&S Market — When to Expect It

Some trades consistently land in the Excess & Surplus (E&S) market because admitted carriers won't write them at standard terms:

| Trade/Scope | E&S Market Likely? | What to Verify |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial roofing (torch-applied, BUR) | Yes — often | Carrier must still be A- rated by AM Best; verify policy form exclusions |
| Steel erection | Yes — high-rise | Same as above |
| Blasting / demolition | Yes | Financial stability of E&S carrier |
| Bridge/industrial painting | Yes | Same as above |
| Asbestos/lead abatement | Yes | Pollution policy is primary vehicle |

**E&S is not disqualifying — but verify:** (1) Carrier AM Best rating A- minimum, (2) No exclusions that leave GC exposed, (3) Occurrence basis preferred over claims-made for long-tail trades.

## Part VII: Bond Requirements

A surety bond from a **Treasury-listed (T-List) surety**, dated within 90 days, is the gold standard prequalification confirmation. The letter must confirm both:
- **Single project bonding capacity** (specific to the proposed project value)
- **Aggregate bonding capacity** (total outstanding bonds the surety will support)

**If a surety will not provide a bondability letter for the proposed project size — this is the most powerful negative signal in the entire prequalification framework.**

| Trade | Typical Single Project Limit | Typical Aggregate Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Electrical | 8–12x working capital | 12–18x working capital |
| HVAC/Plumbing | 8–12x working capital | 12–18x working capital |
| Roofing | 7–10x working capital | 10–15x working capital |
| Framing | 7–10x working capital | 10–15x working capital |
| Concrete | 8–10x working capital | 12–15x working capital |
| Steel | 7–10x working capital | 10–15x working capital |
