## Part I: Automatic Disqualifiers (Any Trade)

These conditions are disqualifying on most serious prequalification programs regardless of trade:

| Disqualifier | Category | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| EMR exceeds hard cutoff | Safety | > 1.25 on most programs; > 1.40 for roofing/framing |
| TRIR exceeds hard cutoff | Safety | > 4.0 (general); trade-adjusted thresholds in Trade-Benchmarks-Master.md |
| Current Ratio below floor | Financial | < 1.1 |
| Bankruptcy in past 7 years | Legal/Character | Any filing |
| Active contract termination for cause | Legal/Character | Any |
| Surety will not provide bondability letter for proposed project size | Financial/Character | Any |
| Trade license lapsed or suspended | Regulatory | Any |
| GL carrier rated below A- by AM Best | Insurance | Any |
| Net Profit Margin below 2% | Financial | Thin margin = job borrowing risk |
| Days of Cash on Hand below 10 days | Financial | Cannot sustain operations without new cash inflows |
| Fatality in the past 3 years with low TRIR | Safety | Suggests underreporting — investigate immediately |

## Part II: Universal Financial Red Flags (All Trades)

### WIP / Billing Red Flags
| Red Flag | Signal | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Profit fade across multiple projects** | Downward revision of estimated gross profit on 2+ active jobs | #1 predictor of subcontractor financial distress |
| **Underbillings > 15% of working capital** | Work performed but not invoiced exceeds normal | Unapproved change orders, billing disputes, or disguised losses |
| **Low Cash-to-Overbillings ratio** | Overbilled cash not retained | Job borrowing — using overbilled cash to fund other projects |
| **Backlog > 10x Working Capital** | Too much work relative to financial resources | High probability of cash flow failure mid-project |
| **Months in Backlog < 3 or > 18** | Desperate for work OR overextended | Underbidding risk OR inability to staff the proposed project |

### Balance Sheet Red Flags
| Red Flag | Signal | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **AR Days > 75** | Slow collections | Working with slow-paying GCs or experiencing collection problems |
| **AP Days significantly > 33** | Slow payments to suppliers | Financial distress — suppliers will pull credit |
| **Debt-to-Equity > 3.0x** | Highly leveraged | Vulnerable to cost overruns or payment delays |
| **Revenue-to-Equity > 15x** | Overtrading on thin equity | Collapse risk if a single project performs poorly |
| **Fixed Asset Ratio > 50%** | Over-invested in depreciating assets | Illiquid balance sheet |

### Revenue Red Flags
| Red Flag | Signal | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue growth > 30% YoY without headcount growth** | Scaling faster than management can handle | Quality and schedule control breakdown risk |
| **Revenue decline > 20% YoY** | Loss of key clients or market position | Investigate before awarding |
| **Heavy concentration with single GC (>40% of backlog)** | Dependency risk | Default risk if that GC relationship ends |

## Part III: Trade-Specific Red Flags

### Electrical (NAICS 238210)
| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Underbillings > 15% of backlog | Material-heavy work should be front-loaded; high underbillings signal billing problems |
| AP to material suppliers past 60 days | Copper/conduit suppliers cut credit quickly — supply disruption risk |
| Revenue growth without licensed journeyman headcount growth | Can't perform the work with unlicensed labor |
| License held by single non-owner individual | Key-person risk — if they leave, license lapses |
| Misclassification of 7538 (line work) as 5190 (inside work) | WC fraud or error; 7538 is 2.5x the cost of 5190 |
| Low TRIR with any electrocution fatality | Suggests underreporting of non-fatal incidents |

### HVAC / Plumbing (NAICS 238220)
| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Fabrication shop not separately coded | Manufacturing exposure requires separate WC class code |
| Missing CPL where refrigerants are handled | Refrigerant release = environmental liability |
| Fire sprinkler work without separate limits | Fire sprinkler is a separate, higher-risk class — standard limits insufficient |
| No Professional Liability for design-assist mechanical scope | Covers E&O on mechanical system design |

### Roofing (NAICS 238160)
| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| No warranty reserve on balance sheet | Should carry 2–5% of revenue; absence = future liability unfunded |
| WC policy with "roofing exclusion" | Some carriers exclude certain roofing operations — creates uncovered liability |
| GL policy with non-admitted carrier below A- | No guarantee of claim payment |
| Completed operations limited to 2 years | Commercial roofing warranties run 10–20 years — 2-year policy leaves GC exposed |
| TRIR below 1.5 on commercial roofing | Well-documented underreporting problem; scrutinize carefully |
| Mix of storm-chasing residential and commercial new construction | Completely different risk profiles; demand separate history |

### Concrete (NAICS 238110)
| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Low equipment on balance sheet relative to revenue | All rented/leased = lower asset backing for bonding; higher cost volatility |
| No CPL if working near waterways | Concrete washout and pump operations = environmental liability |

### Steel Erection (NAICS 238120)
| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Crane operator without NCCCO certification | OSHA 1926.1302 compliance requirement; also underwriting requirement |
| No Riggers Liability coverage | Crane operations are the primary GL loss driver for steel subs |
| NCCI code 5069 with inadequate working capital | WC premiums alone at $60.61/100 can be a financial stress point on long projects |
| Inadequate OSHA 1926 Subpart R documentation | Steel erection safety plan is legally required; absence = compliance failure |

### Framing (NAICS 238130)
| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Fixed-price contracts during lumber price volatility | Lumber price swings of 50–100% have occurred; fixed-price = full exposure |
| No material escalation clauses in contracts | Same as above — verify contract terms |
| EMR > 1.10 | Framing industry average already exceeds 1.0; > 1.10 is above the adjusted threshold |

### Masonry (NAICS 238140)
| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| No documented silica compliance program | OSHA 1926.1153 violation risk; also creates GC liability |
| No medical surveillance records | Required under OSHA silica standard |
| No wet methods or engineering controls documented | OSHA requirement for crystalline silica |

### Painting (NAICS 238320)
| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| No CPL for pre-1978 building work | Lead paint abatement creates environmental liability |
| Bridge/industrial work without 5037 class code | Bridge painting (5037, ~$29.65) coded as commercial interior (5040, ~$10.48) = fraud or error |
| No CPL for solvent-based or epoxy coatings | Chemical exposure and VOC liability |

### Site Preparation (NAICS 238910)
| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| No CPL | Environmental liability is nearly certain for excavation/underground work |
| No underground utility locate confirmation | Utility strikes are a major liability and schedule risk |
| Equipment leased without contingency plan | Heavy equipment availability directly determines schedule performance |

## Part IV: Insurance-Specific Red Flags (All Trades)

| Red Flag | Implication |
|---|---|
| Carrier not on AM Best A- / VII list | No guarantee of financial ability to pay claims |
| Policy on claims-made basis for long-tail trade | Roofing, steel, fire sprinkler need occurrence basis — claims-made creates sunset risk |
| Shared aggregate across multiple projects | One large claim on another project can exhaust the aggregate |
| Completed ops coverage sunset at 2 years | Insufficient for roofing, fire sprinkler, steel |
| COI shows coverage but no endorsements provided | Certificate ≠ policy; require actual endorsements for high-risk trades |
| Non-admitted carrier for Extreme-tier trades | E&S is acceptable only if carrier is A- rated and policy has no critical exclusions |

## Part V: Safety Red Flags (All Trades)

| Red Flag | Implication |
|---|---|
| EMR trending upward over 3 years | Worsening safety culture — lagging indicator of systemic problems |
| OSHA willful violations in past 5 years | Indicates deliberate disregard for worker safety |
| Fatality in past 3 years | High-severity indicator; investigate circumstances and corrective action |
| TRIR well below trade average but DART is high | Few incidents but severe when they occur; or underreporting of minor injuries |
| No designated Safety Officer | Indicates safety is not a management priority |
| OSHA 300 logs not signed by company executive | Non-compliance with OSHA recordkeeping requirement |
| Drug and alcohol testing policy absent | Particularly high risk for trades operating heavy equipment |

## Part VI: Character Red Flags

| Red Flag | Implication |
|---|---|
| Mechanic's liens filed against prior projects | Indicates payment problems with suppliers or sub-subs — downstream liability for GC |
| UCC filings indicating multiple secured creditors | Assets are encumbered; financial distress signal |
| Active litigation with a prior GC | May indicate pattern of disputes; also a financial drag |
| Prior GC references who will not recommend rehire | Most powerful qualitative negative signal |
| Bank reference letter declined or qualified | Bank is the most informed creditor; a weak reference = severe warning |
| Self-reported EMR that doesn't match carrier verification | Fraud — disqualify immediately |
