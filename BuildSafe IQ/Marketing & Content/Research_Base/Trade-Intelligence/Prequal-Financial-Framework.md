_The 3 Cs, CFMA Benchmarks, WIP Analysis, and Complete Data Point Checklist._
_Sources: CFMA 2024 Financial Benchmarker, Marsh SubSecure, AIA A305, NASBP, IRMI_
_Last updated: 2026-05-12_

## Part I: The 3 Cs Framework

Per CFMA: *"Subcontractor prequalification criteria are akin to surety bond underwriting. To determine whether to prequalify a subcontractor, gather information from the three 'Cs' — capacity, capital, and character."*

| C | Core Question | Primary Data Sources |
|---|---|---|
| **Capital** | Can they sustain operations financially and absorb project setbacks without defaulting? | Financial statements, WIP schedule, AR/AP aging, bank letter, bonding capacity |
| **Capacity** | Do they have the operational infrastructure, workforce, and experience to execute this specific scope? | Project history, headcount, equipment schedule, QMP, references |
| **Character** | Have they behaved reliably, safely, and ethically — and will they continue to? | Safety records, litigation history, payment practices, regulatory compliance |

## Part II: Financial Documentation Requirements by Contract Value

| Contract Value | Required Financial Statement Level | Supporting Documents |
|---|---|---|
| Under $500K | Company-prepared statements | Basic financials |
| $500K – $2M | CPA-reviewed (SSARS Review) | WIP, AR/AP aging, bank letter |
| $2M – $5M | CPA-reviewed or audited | All above + interim financials (within 90 days) |
| Over $5M | Fully audited (GAAS) | All above + bonding capacity letter, credit report |

## Part III: CFMA Benchmark Tables (2024 Financial Benchmarker)

### Liquidity Ratios

| Metric | Formula | CFMA Specialty Trade | Best in Class | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Current Ratio** | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | ~1.6 | > 2.0 | < 1.1 |
| **Quick Ratio** | (Cash + Securities + AR) / Current Liabilities | ~1.4 | > 1.8 | < 1.0 |
| **Days of Cash on Hand** | Cash / ((Oper. Exp. – Depreciation) / 365) | ~22 days | > 40 days | < 10 days |
| **Underbillings to Working Capital** | Underbillings / Working Capital | ~8% | < 5% | > 25% |

**Key Insight:** Days of Cash = ~3 weeks for the average specialty trade firm. Awarding a large contract to a firm with <10 days of cash means the GC is effectively providing an unsecured line of credit.

### Profitability Ratios

| Metric | Formula | CFMA Specialty Trade | Best in Class | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Gross Profit Margin** | (Revenue – Cost of Sales) / Revenue | ~15–16% | 21.8% | < 10% |
| **Net Profit Margin (Pre-Tax)** | Net Income Before Tax / Revenue | 6.9% | 11.9% | < 2% |
| **Return on Assets (ROA)** | Net Income / Total Assets | 13.4% | 28.4% | < 5% |
| **Return on Equity (ROE)** | Net Income / Total Equity | 31.4% | 59.7% | — |
| **Revenue per FTE** | Total Revenue / FTEs | $450,086 | — | — |

**Key Insight:** Net Profit Margin below 2% = operating on razor-thin margins. High risk of "job borrowing" — using cash from profitable projects to fund losses elsewhere. This is the most common precursor to subcontractor default.

### Leverage Ratios

| Metric | Formula | CFMA All Companies | Best in Class | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Debt to Equity** | Total Liabilities / Total Equity | 1.3x | 0.9x | > 3.0x |
| **Times Interest Earned** | EBIT / Interest Expense | 25.6x | — | < 5.0x |
| **Fixed Asset Ratio** | Fixed Assets / Total Assets | 23.7% | 12.9% | > 50% |
| **Revenue to Equity** | Total Revenue / Total Equity | 5.4x | — | > 15x |

### Efficiency and Backlog Ratios

| Metric | Formula | CFMA All Companies | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Days in Accounts Receivable** | (AR / Revenue) × 365 | 56.6 days | > 75 days |
| **Days in Accounts Payable** | (AP / Revenue) × 365 | 33.0 days | — |
| **Operating Cycle** | Days AR + Days Inventory – Days AP | 52.7 days | — |
| **Months in Backlog** | (Backlog / Annual Revenue) × 12 | 8.7 months | < 3 or > 18 months |
| **Backlog to Working Capital** | Backlog / Working Capital | 5.9x | > 10x |
| **Average Backlog to Equity** | Average Backlog / Total Equity | 4.4x | — |

## Part IV: WIP Schedule Analysis

**The WIP schedule is the most important document in subcontractor financial prequalification.** Standard financial statements cannot reveal real-time project health; WIP can.

### Required WIP Data Points (Per Active Project)

- Contract value (including all approved change orders)
- Revised estimated cost at completion
- Costs incurred to date
- Estimated cost to complete
- Percentage complete (cost method)
- Billings to date
- Overbillings (Billings in Excess of Costs + Estimated Earnings)
- Underbillings (Costs + Estimated Earnings in Excess of Billings)
- Estimated gross profit at completion
- Original estimated gross profit at bid
- **Profit fade** = (Original GP% – Current estimated GP%)

### Critical WIP Metrics

| Metric | How to Calculate | Red Flag Threshold | What It Signals |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Profit Fade** | Original GP estimate – Current GP estimate, across all active jobs | Consistent fade on 2+ projects | #1 predictor of financial distress. Firm is either a poor estimator or losing control of field costs. |
| **Underbillings to Working Capital** | Underbillings / Working Capital | > 15% | Unapproved change orders (collection risk), billing delays, or disguised project losses |
| **Cash to Overbillings Ratio** | Cash / Overbillings | < 0.50 | Overbilled cash has been spent — firm is using future receipts to fund current operations ("job borrowing") |
| **Backlog to Working Capital** | Total Backlog / Working Capital | > 10x | Attempting to execute far more work than financial resources can support |

## Part V: Advanced Risk Scoring — Marsh SubSecure / SDI Framework

For GCs using SDI (Subcontractor Default Insurance) or high-deductible programs, subcontractors are scored across 10 financial dimensions:

| # | Dimension | Key Metrics |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Liquidity | Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, Days of Cash |
| 2 | Working Capital | Absolute working capital adequacy for proposed project |
| 3 | Underbillings | Underbillings exposure relative to working capital |
| 4 | Overbillings | Cash-to-overbillings ratio |
| 5 | Profitability | Gross Margin trend, Net Margin stability |
| 6 | Leverage | Debt-to-Equity, Times Interest Earned |
| 7 | Backlog | Backlog relative to capacity and working capital |
| 8 | AR Quality | Days in AR, aging distribution, concentration |
| 9 | Cash Flow | Cash flow from operations vs. net income |
| 10 | Balance Sheet | Equity trend, overall financial strength |

**Output:** Score in 4 quartiles representing probability of default.

**ROI of Prequalification:**
- Reduces WC claims piercing GC deductible
- Reduces GL claims from subcontractor negligence
- Reduces SDI deductible triggers
- Improves GC's own EMR over time → lower WC premiums
- Strengthens GC surety relationship

## Part VI: Complete Data Point Checklist

### A — Company Identity
- [ ] Legal entity name and all DBA names
- [ ] FEIN / IRS W-9
- [ ] State of incorporation + good standing certificate
- [ ] Year founded, years under current ownership
- [ ] Ownership structure (all owners >5%)
- [ ] Key executives (President, CFO, Project Executive)
- [ ] NAICS code(s) for primary work

### B — Licensing and Regulatory Compliance
- [ ] All active trade licenses (number, state, expiration, certifying agency)
- [ ] Specialty certifications: NFPA, EPA 608, ASME, NCCCO, NICET, NABCEP (as applicable)
- [ ] Union affiliations and CBA currency (benefit fund contributions current?)
- [ ] OSHA violation history (5 years: citations, penalties, abatement)
- [ ] Labor law violations (FLSA, Davis-Bacon, prevailing wage)
- [ ] Environmental compliance history

### C — Financial Data
- [ ] Audited or CPA-reviewed financials (2–3 fiscal years)
- [ ] Most recent interim financials (within 90 days)
- [ ] WIP Schedule (current, all active projects)
- [ ] AR aging schedule (0–30, 31–60, 61–90, 90+ days)
- [ ] AP aging schedule
- [ ] Bank line of credit confirmation letter (available balance, current outstanding)
- [ ] Bonding capacity letter (single project + aggregate, T-List surety, within 90 days)
- [ ] Business credit report (D&B, Experian, or Equifax Business)
- [ ] UCC lien search
- [ ] Mechanic's lien history on prior projects (public record)

**Financial Ratios to Calculate and Score:**
- [ ] Current Ratio — benchmark ≥ 1.4
- [ ] Quick Ratio — benchmark ≥ 1.2
- [ ] Days of Cash on Hand — benchmark ≥ 15 days
- [ ] Working Capital (absolute) — adequate for proposed project
- [ ] Underbillings to Working Capital — benchmark < 15%
- [ ] Gross Profit Margin — benchmark ≥ 10% (trade-adjusted)
- [ ] Net Profit Margin — benchmark ≥ 3%; red flag < 2%
- [ ] Return on Assets — benchmark ≥ 8%
- [ ] Debt to Equity — benchmark ≤ 2.5x
- [ ] Times Interest Earned — benchmark ≥ 5x
- [ ] Days in AR — benchmark ≤ 65 days
- [ ] Months in Backlog — benchmark 3–15 months
- [ ] Backlog to Working Capital — benchmark ≤ 8x; red flag > 10x
- [ ] WIP Profit Fade analysis (current estimated GP vs. original)
- [ ] Cash to Overbillings ratio

### D — Safety Data
- [ ] EMR (3 years, verified from WC carrier — not self-reported)
- [ ] OSHA 300 Logs (3–5 years)
- [ ] OSHA 300A Summaries (3–5 years, signed by executive)
- [ ] TRIR calculation — benchmark ≤ 2.0 (trade-adjusted)
- [ ] DART Rate calculation — benchmark ≤ 1.5 (trade-adjusted)
- [ ] OSHA citations count and type (past 5 years)
- [ ] Fatality history
- [ ] Written Safety and Health Program (OSHA 1926 compliant)
- [ ] Site-Specific Safety Plan capability
- [ ] Fall Protection Program
- [ ] Confined Space Entry Program (if applicable)
- [ ] Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) Program (if applicable)
- [ ] Drug and Alcohol Testing Policy
- [ ] Safety training records (OSHA 10/30, First Aid/CPR)
- [ ] Safety Officer qualifications and certifications

### E — Insurance
- [ ] COI with all required endorsements
- [ ] CGL: $1M/$2M minimum; carrier A- / VII minimum
- [ ] WC: Statutory; carrier rated
- [ ] Employers' Liability: $1M / $1M / $1M
- [ ] Commercial Auto: $1M CSL
- [ ] Excess/Umbrella: $5M–$25M per project requirement
- [ ] Professional Liability (if design-assist scope)
- [ ] Pollution Liability (if environmental scope)
- [ ] Additional Insured endorsements: CG 20 10 + CG 20 37
- [ ] Primary and Non-Contributory endorsement
- [ ] Waiver of Subrogation (all lines)
- [ ] No gaps or lapses confirmed
- [ ] Trade-specific specialty coverages (see Trade-Coverage-Matrix.md)

### F — Capacity and Operations
- [ ] Largest successfully completed project (value + description)
- [ ] 5–10 most relevant completed projects with GC references
- [ ] Annual revenue (3 years)
- [ ] Current backlog (value + active project list)
- [ ] Total headcount: field vs. office
- [ ] Labor sourcing: self-perform vs. brokered vs. sub-subcontracted
- [ ] Union CBA status and benefit fund contributions
- [ ] Key personnel resumes: Project Manager + Superintendent
- [ ] OSHA 30-hour certifications for all supervisory staff
- [ ] Equipment schedule (owned and leased, with age and condition)
- [ ] Sub-subcontractor management process
- [ ] Quality Management Plan (written)
- [ ] Project management software / BIM capabilities

### G — Character and Reputation
- [ ] Litigation disclosure (active and past 5 years)
- [ ] Contract default and termination for cause history
- [ ] Bankruptcy history (past 7 years)
- [ ] Judgment history
- [ ] Reference checks: minimum 3 GC references, called directly (do not accept written references only)
- [ ] Supplier and sub-subcontractor references
- [ ] Banking relationship reference letter
- [ ] Payment practices reputation (lien history search)
- [ ] Surety relationship history and current bondability status

## Part VII: The Surety Bond as Ultimate Prequalification Tool

*"A surety company that has independently underwritten a subcontractor and issued a performance and payment bond has already conducted the most rigorous prequalification process available."*

If a sub cannot obtain a surety bond for the proposed project:
- This is the most powerful negative signal in the entire framework
- It overrides positive financial ratios, good safety records, and strong references
- Investigate immediately before any award decision

**Bond Types:**
- **Bid Bond:** Guarantees the sub will enter into a contract at the bid price
- **Performance Bond:** Guarantees completion of the work per contract terms
- **Payment Bond:** Guarantees payment to suppliers and sub-subs (protects GC from liens)
