# Construction Starts Rebound 13% in March

**Source:** Construction.com | **Date:** April 17, 2026

## Main Finding
Total construction starts jumped 12.8% in March to an annual rate of $1.22 trillion. Year-to-date remains slightly negative at -0.5%, so the rebound is real but hasn't yet overcome the soft start to 2026.

## Sector Breakdown
| Sector | Monthly Change | Annual Change |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Nonresidential building | +6.3% | — |
| Residential | +2.6% | Single-family -15.7% |
| Nonbuilding (infrastructure) | +37.9% | — |
| Commercial | -9.2% | +19.2% |
| Office/Data Centers | -16.0% | — |
| Manufacturing | +251.9% | +20.2% |

## Risk Implications
- Volatility is the story — month-to-month swings this large signal unpredictability in project pipelines
- Office/data center pullback (-16% monthly) creates near-term risk exposure for GCs in that segment
- Manufacturing rebound is dramatic but concentrated — not a broad market recovery
- Residential fragility (single-family -15.7% annually) reflects housing market stress that could dampen subcontractor demand

## Relevance to BuildSafe IQ
Market volatility = higher risk complexity = stronger case for risk intelligence platforms. Segments with dramatic swings (manufacturing, data centers) are exactly where GCs need better project-level risk scoring.
