## Synthesis

NYC is showing a bifurcated construction signal entering spring 2026. Monthly construction starts rose in March across multiple sectors, including the $420 million 61 Broadway office-to-residential conversion — but the Dodge Construction Network data is explicit that annual totals are still lagging the prior year's pace. The city is recovering, not recovered. Meanwhile, the debt side is moving faster than the permit/starts side: The Real Deal's March debt pipeline reporting shows lenders actively putting money to work in residential and office projects across Manhattan and Brooklyn, and Empire State Realty Trust's earnings guidance signals the office recovery is generating investable returns. Financing availability typically leads construction activity by 6–12 months.

The typology mix matters. The leading starts are affordable housing (ENY Phase 3 — government-subsidized, subsidy-driven timeline) and adaptive reuse (61 Broadway — office-to-residential conversion). Neither is speculative market-rate construction. Affordable housing projects operate under different contracting structures — HPD oversight, Davis-Bacon wage requirements, Section 3 hiring mandates, and layered financing from multiple public sources that create complex change-order approval chains. Office-to-residential conversion is a structurally different risk profile than ground-up: contractors inherit existing structural conditions, discover hidden deficiencies during demolition, and face scope volatility at rates substantially higher than new construction. The $420M 61 Broadway budget is likely sized with contingency, but adaptive reuse contingencies are routinely insufficient for older NYC office stock.

The non-obvious implication for BSIQ: the debt pipeline surge is the most important leading indicator in this batch. If NYC construction lending surged in March 2026, projects that closed financing are now moving through preconstruction — permitting, design development, GMP negotiation. That cohort of projects will be breaking ground in Q3–Q4 2026. For BSIQ's sales pipeline, NYC-based GCs and developers currently in preconstruction on office-to-residential conversions are an actionable near-term target segment — they're about to commit to budgets and risk programs on projects with above-average change order exposure.
