# Stagflation — What It Could Mean for Construction and Surety Markets

**Source:** Construction.com | **Date:** October 17, 2025

## Main Argument
Stagflation isn't the base case but it's a credible tail risk. Construction and surety professionals should be stress-testing portfolios now, not waiting for confirmation.

## Key Risk Scenarios

**Private Construction:**
- Tighter credit + elevated costs + weakened consumer spending = pressure on residential and commercial starts
- Profitability squeeze as materials inflation outpaces contract pricing

**Surety Exposure:**
- Contractor defaults typically rise in stagflationary environments
- Requires more sophisticated risk assessment and portfolio diversification
- Surety carriers with heavy concentration in private commercial face asymmetric downside

**Infrastructure Resilience:**
- Public/government-funded projects are the hedge — federally funded infrastructure may weather the cycle better

## Recommended Actions (from article)
- Monitor cost pressures continuously
- Diversify project types across public/private
- Build contractual flexibility (escalation clauses, material cost provisions)
