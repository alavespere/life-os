# Next-Generation Nuclear: Global Trends, Risks and Opportunities for Insurers

**Source:** Clyde & Co LLP | **Date:** April 30, 2026

## Main Argument
Nuclear power is re-emerging as a critical energy pillar driven by AI electricity demand, decarbonization pressure, and geopolitical energy security concerns. Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) are reshaping how nuclear gets built, financed, and insured. Insurers who get into this market early face significant opportunity across construction, liability, transport, and operational lines.

## US Market
- Data center electricity demand from tech giants is driving renewed nuclear interest — direct power-purchase agreements tied to nuclear plants are becoming a real market
- Many states still have nuclear moratoria even while pursuing clean energy targets — legislative carve-outs for advanced reactors are in progress
- Price-Anderson Act updated to cover SMRs with proportional liability requirements scaled to smaller installations

## Global Highlights
| Country | Status | Key Insurance Angle |
|---------|--------|-------------------|
| Poland | First-of-a-kind nuclear; AP1000 + SMRs | Construction/erection risk, no operational history |
| France | Mature fleet; rolling out EPR2 + SMR R&D | Stable Paris/Brussels Convention regime |
| Netherlands | 14.5 GW nuclear target by 2050 | Coordinated national nuclear insurance pool |
| UK | Civil Nuclear Roadmap to 2050; first SMR site at Wylfa announced 2026 | Project risk, construction, liability lines |

## Insurance Opportunities
- Completion and construction risk (first-of-a-kind projects = no historical loss data)
- Supply chain and transport cover
- Decommissioning guarantees
- Liability frameworks scaled to reactor size (especially for SMRs)
