# Using AI to Transform Construction Safety

**Source:** Procore Library | **Authors:** Hugh Seaton, Kristen Frisa

## Main Argument
Construction is still the deadliest sector for US workers. AI can meaningfully reduce fatalities through real-time hazard detection, predictive analytics, and automated compliance monitoring — but human judgment remains essential.

## Five AI Applications in Construction Safety

| Application | How It Works |
|-------------|-------------|
| Robotic Automation | Robots perform high-hazard tasks (demolition, excavation), eliminating human exposure |
| Predictive Analytics | Historical data forecasts high-risk conditions before incidents occur |
| Jobsite Security | Drones + smart cameras provide continuous monitoring for unsafe practices |
| Real-Time Hazard Detection | AI analyzes photos/video for PPE non-compliance and environmental hazards |
| Automated Reporting | AI reviews documentation for HSE and regulatory compliance |

## Key Caveat
"AI complements, but doesn't substitute for, human work." — Hugh Seaton. Over-automation risks worker disengagement and productivity loss.

## Limitations Noted
- No specific incident reduction percentages or ROI metrics cited
- Predictive analytics requires large camera-captured training datasets — barrier for smaller GCs
- Article is advocacy-oriented, not peer-reviewed
