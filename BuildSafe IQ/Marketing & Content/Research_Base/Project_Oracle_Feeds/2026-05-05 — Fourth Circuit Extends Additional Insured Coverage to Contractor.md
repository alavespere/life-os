# Fourth Circuit Extends Coverage to Contractor

**Source:** Lexology — Hunton Andrews Kurth LLP
**Date:** May 5, 2026
**Case:** *APAC-Atlantic, Inc. v. Owners Insurance Co.*, No. 24-1969, 2026 WL 458402 (4th Cir. Feb. 18, 2026)

---

## Synthesis

The Fourth Circuit issued a pro-coverage ruling interpreting "arising out of" language in additional insured endorsements broadly under North Carolina law. In *APAC-Atlantic, Inc. v. Owners Insurance Co.*, the court held that a repaving GC was entitled to coverage under its subcontractor's liability policy because the additional insured's liability "related to" or was "causally connected to" the named insured's work — not limited to liability directly "caused by" or "the fault of" the subcontractor. The ruling applies to standard ISO additional-insured endorsement language that has evolved over time, with courts interpreting different versions with varying degrees of narrowness.

This ruling matters directly for construction risk. When a GC requires subcontractors to name them as additional insureds on their CGL policies, the scope of that coverage hinges entirely on how courts interpret "arising out of" in the endorsement. A broad reading — as the Fourth Circuit adopted here — means the GC can access the sub's limits even when the GC itself was partially at fault, as long as the sub's work is in the causal chain. Tighter endorsement language or different state law can flip that result entirely, which is why contractual risk transfer strategies need to be reviewed jurisdiction by jurisdiction.

The non-obvious implication: endorsement vintage matters. ISO has tightened additional insured language over time — earlier versions said "arising out of" while later versions say "caused by acts or omissions" of the named insured. GCs and their brokers need to audit whether the AI endorsements being collected from subs use legacy broad language or the newer narrow language, because the Fourth Circuit's ruling only helps if the right version of the endorsement is in place. This is a live underwriting differentiator that most GCs are not actively managing.
