# Building Safety Culture from the Bottom Up — Knutson Construction

**Source:** Procore Library | **Author:** Kevin Hogan, Corporate Safety Director, Knutson Construction

## Main Argument
Top-down compliance-focused safety programs fail because field crews find workarounds. Effective safety requires involving workers in program design and measuring "great catches" (proactive hazard reporting) rather than punishing incidents.

## Key Insights
- Hogan uses competitive KPIs on live dashboards to incentivize proactive hazard reporting
- Redundant paperwork (overlapping FLRAs and permits) creates compliance failures — streamlining is a risk control, not just efficiency
- Traditional reactive/disciplinary approaches have not reduced serious injuries or fatalities
- Field crews view impractical safety programs as obstacles, not protection

## Risk Management Implications
- Engagement gap is a leading indicator: sites where workers distrust the safety program are at elevated risk regardless of their paperwork compliance
- Streamlined, worker-designed processes reduce actual violations — not just reported ones
- "Great catches" metric is a better leading indicator than TRIR for underwriters assessing culture

## Relevance to BuildSafe IQ
Safety culture quality is an underwriting signal that doesn't show up in OSHA logs. A risk scoring model that captures culture indicators (worker engagement, reporting rates, program simplicity) would differentiate BuildSafe IQ from carriers relying on lagging indicators alone.
