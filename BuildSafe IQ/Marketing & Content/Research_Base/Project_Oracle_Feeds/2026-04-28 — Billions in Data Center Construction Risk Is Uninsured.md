# Report: Billions of Dollars in Data Center Construction Risk Is Uninsured

**Source:** Engineering News-Record — Bryan Gottlieb
**Date:** April 28, 2026
**Key sources:** S&P Global Ratings (April 13 analyst report), Swiss Re, Marsh (Sedat Kunt, national builders' risk practice leader), DPR Construction (Amy Iannone), Aon, FM Global

---

## Synthesis

Annual data center investment could exceed $300 billion by 2027, with individual hyperscale campuses now reaching $10–$25 billion in insurable value per site. The insurance market cannot keep pace: instead of insuring to full replacement value, underwriters are now using Probable Maximum Loss (PML) modeling, which typically results in policy limits of $1.5–$3.5 billion on a $10 billion project — leaving one-third to one-half of project value uninsured, per S&P Global Ratings. The coverage gap is compounded by delay-in-startup (business interruption) insurance, which when required causes capacity to shrink further because lost rental income on a hyperscale campus can approach construction cost dollar-for-dollar. Swiss Re estimates the global premium pool tied to data centers will grow from $10.6 billion today to $24.2 billion by 2030. New facility structures are emerging — Marsh's Nimbus program (launched June 2025) offers up to $2.7 billion in limits inclusive of BI; Aon expanded its Data Center Lifecycle Insurance Program to $3.5 billion in April 2026.

The coverage gap is not the only risk signal. More than 40% of U.S. data center capacity is located in tornado-exposed regions, and over one-quarter faces substantial hail risk — both of which drive PML modeling upward and compress available limits. Co-location of multiple hyperscale campuses in the same geographic corridors (Northern Virginia, Phoenix, etc.) creates aggregation risk that a single catastrophic event could simultaneously stress builders' risk, workers' comp, and liability programs held by the same carrier across co-located sites. S&P estimates a single data center site could generate close to $10 billion in natural catastrophe losses — roughly 9% of total global insured catastrophe losses in 2025. The industry has not yet seen a major loss at hyperscale levels, but carriers and market observers acknowledge this is a fragility that hasn't been tested.

The non-obvious implication for BSIQ: data center construction is creating a new class of owner-retained risk — billions in uninsured exposure sitting on hyperscaler balance sheets or flowing through captive structures. This is exactly the kind of structured risk management problem that creates demand for third-party risk intelligence and analytics. Owners retaining $5–$15 billion in self-insured exposure per campus need sophisticated loss modeling, not just standard insurance procurement. BSIQ's risk scoring platform, if extended to data center construction typologies, could serve this market directly — and the wholesale broker distribution channel (Burns & Wilcox, AmWINS, RT Specialty) is already active in this space placing the layered programs. This is a near-term market expansion opportunity worth tracking.
