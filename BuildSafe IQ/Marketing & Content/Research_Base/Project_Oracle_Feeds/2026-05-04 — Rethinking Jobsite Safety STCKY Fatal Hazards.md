# Rethinking Jobsite Safety — The Fatal 8 and STCKY Hazard Walks

**Source:** Procore Library | **Date:** 2026

## Main Argument
Recordable injury rates are a misleading safety metric. Fatality numbers haven't moved since ~2007 despite falling incident rates. The real problem: organizations aren't identifying and controlling the specific hazards that kill people. STCKY (the stuff that can kill you) reframes safety around life-threatening exposures.

## Key Data Points
- Fatality plateau: numbers unchanged since ~2007 despite recordable injury declines
- 16% of STCKY™ Walks on well-managed projects identified missing controls
- Projects using this approach saw measurable reductions in serious incidents and insurance claims

## The Fatal 8 High-Risk Categories
1. Lifts
2. Working at heights
3. Underground construction
4. Confined spaces
5. Energy storage devices
6. Hazardous materials
7. Moving/crushing equipment
8. Uncontrolled energy scenarios

## Risk Management Implications
- Metric mismatch: low TRIR ≠ low fatality risk — underwriters relying on recordable rates alone are missing the signal
- Control gap: 16% missing-control rate on "well-managed" sites means the average site is significantly worse
- Insurance claim reduction is a direct financial outcome of better hazard identification

## Relevance to BuildSafe IQ
This is directly relevant to risk scoring methodology. A contractor with low TRIR but weak controls on Fatal 8 categories is a worse risk than their incident rate suggests. BuildSafe IQ's scoring should incorporate hazard-category exposure, not just aggregate safety stats.
