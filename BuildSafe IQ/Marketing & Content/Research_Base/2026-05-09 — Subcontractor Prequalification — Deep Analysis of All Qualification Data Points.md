## Based on CFMA Benchmarks, Surety Underwriting Standards, and Industry Best Practices

**Prepared by:** BuildSafe IQ Research Division  
**Date:** April 2026  
**Classification:** Professional Reference Document

---

## Executive Summary

The failure of a subcontractor to perform can be one of the most financially damaging events a General Contractor (GC) can experience. Delays, cost overruns, defective work, lien claims, and cascading project failures are all direct consequences of engaging an unqualified specialty trade contractor.

This document provides an exhaustive analysis of every data point used to determine whether a subcontractor is qualified to work a job. The evaluation framework is organized around the **"3 Cs of Prequalification"** — **Capacity, Capital, and Character** — which mirror surety bond underwriting criteria.

---

## Part I: The Framework — The 3 Cs of Prequalification

Per CFMA's published guidance: *"Essentially, subcontractor prequalification criteria are akin to that of the surety bond underwriting process. To determine whether to prequalify a subcontractor, contractors should gather information from each of the three 'Cs' — capacity, capital, and character."*

| C | Definition | Key Data Points |
|---|---|---|
| **Capital** | Financial stability — can they sustain operations and absorb setbacks? | Financial statements, WIP, liquidity ratios, backlog |
| **Capacity** | Operational infrastructure — can they actually execute this scope? | Project history, workforce, equipment, QMP |
| **Character** | Historical behavior and reputation — will they perform ethically? | Safety record, litigation history, payment practices |

## Part II: Capital — Financial Stability and Benchmarks

### 2.1 Required Financial Documents by Contract Value

| Contract Value | Required Financial Statement Level |
|---|---|
| Under $500,000 | Company-prepared financial statements |
| $500,000 – $2,000,000 | CPA-reviewed financial statements (SSARS Review) |
| $2,000,000 – $5,000,000 | CPA-reviewed or audited financial statements |
| Over $5,000,000 | Fully audited financial statements (GAAS Audit) |

**Supporting documents required at all levels:**
- Work in Progress (WIP) Schedule
- Accounts Receivable aging schedule (0–30, 31–60, 61–90, 90+ days)
- Accounts Payable aging schedule
- Bank line of credit confirmation letter
- Most recent interim financial statements (within 90 days for large contracts)

### 2.2 CFMA Liquidity Benchmarks (2024 Financial Benchmarker)

| Liquidity Metric | CFMA 2023 All Companies | Specialty Trade | Best in Class | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Current Ratio** | 1.6 | ~1.6 | > 2.0 | < 1.1 |
| **Quick Ratio** | 1.4 | ~1.4 | > 1.8 | < 1.0 |
| **Days of Cash on Hand** | 23.5 days | ~22 days | > 40 days | < 10 days |
| **Underbillings to Working Capital** | 8.0% | ~8% | < 5% | > 25% |

**Key Insight — Days of Cash:** The CFMA benchmark of 23.5 days means the average firm can operate for only ~3 weeks without new cash inflows. A GC awarding a large contract to a firm with <10 days of cash is essentially providing an unsecured line of credit.

**Key Insight — Underbillings to Working Capital:** Underbillings are an unsecured receivable. When they represent a large percentage of working capital, the firm's liquidity is illusory.

### 2.3 CFMA Profitability Benchmarks

| Profitability Metric | CFMA 2023 All Companies | Specialty Trade | Best in Class |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gross Profit Margin** | ~14.8% | ~15–16% | 21.8% |
| **Net Profit Margin (Pre-Tax)** | 6.3% | 6.9% | 11.9% |
| **Return on Assets (ROA)** | 11.8% | 13.4% | 28.4% |
| **Return on Equity (ROE)** | 31.4% | 31.4% | 59.7% |
| **Revenue per FTE** | $450,086 | — | — |

**Red Flag:** Net Profit Margin below 2% = operating on razor-thin margins with no buffer. High risk of "job borrowing" — using profitable project cash to fund losses elsewhere.

### 2.4 CFMA Leverage Benchmarks

| Leverage Metric | CFMA 2023 All Companies | Best in Class | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Debt to Equity Ratio** | 1.3x | 0.9x | > 3.0x |
| **Times Interest Earned** | 25.6x | — | < 5.0x |
| **Fixed Asset Ratio** | 23.7% | 12.9% | > 50% |
| **Revenue to Equity** | 5.4x | — | > 15x |

### 2.5 CFMA Efficiency & Backlog Benchmarks

| Metric | CFMA 2023 All Companies | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Days in Accounts Receivable** | 56.6 days | > 75 days is a red flag |
| **Days in Accounts Payable** | 33.0 days | |
| **Operating Cycle** | 52.7 days | |
| **Months in Backlog** | 8.7 months | < 3 or > 18 months are red flags |
| **Backlog to Working Capital** | 5.9x | > 10x = high cash flow failure risk |
| **Average Backlog to Equity** | 4.4x | |

### 2.6 WIP Schedule Deep Analysis

**The WIP schedule is the most important document in subcontractor financial prequalification.** Key data points per active project:

- Contract value (including approved change orders)
- Revised estimated cost at completion
- Costs incurred to date / Estimated cost to complete
- Percentage complete (cost method)
- Billings to date
- Overbillings and Underbillings
- Estimated gross profit at completion vs. original estimated gross profit
- **Profit fade** (variance between original and current estimated gross profit)

**Critical WIP Metrics:**

| Metric | What It Signals |
|---|---|
| **Profit Fade** | Consistent downward revisions across multiple projects = #1 predictor of financial distress |
| **Underbillings >8–10% of Working Capital** | Unapproved change orders, billing delays, or disguised project losses |
| **Low Cash-to-Overbillings Ratio** | Overbilled cash has been spent — classic "job borrowing" signal |

## Part III: Capacity — Operational and Technical Evaluation

### 3.1 Project Experience and Scale

**The 1.5–2x Rule:** A subcontractor should not be awarded a contract exceeding **1.5 to 2 times the value of their largest successfully completed project.**

**Project Type Matching Required:**
- Project type (commercial, industrial, healthcare, education, data center)
- Delivery method (DBB, Design-Build, CMaR, IPD)
- Project complexity (occupied renovations, LEED requirements)
- Contract structure (lump sum, GMP, T&M)

**Self-Performance Percentage:** Firms sub-subcontracting >50% of their own scope have limited quality and schedule control.

### 3.2 Workforce Assessment

**Labor Sourcing — Key Distinction:**

| Labor Type | Risk Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Self-performed (W-2) | Lowest | Most reliable for quality and schedule |
| Brokered labor | Moderate | Quality variability, WC exposure |
| Sub-subcontracting | Highest | Reduces GC visibility entirely |

**Key Personnel Requirements:**
- OSHA 30-hour for all supervisory personnel
- Trade certifications: NFPA 70E (electrical), EPA 608 (HVAC)
- Union CBA status and benefit fund contribution currency

### 3.3 Equipment and Technology

- Equipment schedule: owned equipment, age, condition, value
- Project management software (Procore, Viewpoint, Sage)
- BIM/VDC capabilities

### 3.4 Quality Management System

- Written Quality Management Plan (QMP)
- Designated Quality Control Manager qualifications
- Inspection and Test Plans (ITPs)
- History of warranty claims and rework on prior projects
- Direct GC reference checks (minimum 3)

## Part IV: Character — Safety, Compliance, and Reputation

### 4.1 Safety Performance Metrics

**EMR Interpretation Table:**

| EMR Value | Interpretation | Prequalification Status |
|---|---|---|
| < 0.75 | Significantly below average | Preferred subcontractor |
| 0.75 – 1.00 | Below average to average | Standard approval |
| 1.00 | Industry average | Conditional — may require safety plan |
| 1.01 – 1.25 | Above average claims | Written safety improvement plan required |
| > 1.25 | High risk | Automatic disqualification on most programs |

**TRIR Formula:** `(Number of Recordable Incidents × 200,000) / Total Hours Worked`
- BLS average for specialty trades: ~3.0–3.5
- GC typical threshold: ≤ 2.0
- Disqualifying: > 4.0

**DART Formula:** `(Number of DART Cases × 200,000) / Total Hours Worked`
- DART is more meaningful than TRIR — captures severity, not just frequency

**Safety Documentation Required:**
- OSHA 300 Logs (3–5 years)
- OSHA 300A Summaries (3–5 years)
- Written Safety and Health Program
- Fall Protection, Confined Space, LOTO programs
- Drug and Alcohol Testing Policy
- OSHA 10/30 certifications for field personnel

### 4.2 Insurance Coverage Verification

**Required Coverages and Minimum Limits:**

| Coverage | Typical Minimum Limits | Key Endorsements |
|---|---|---|
| **CGL** | $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate | CG 20 10 + CG 20 37; Primary/Non-Contributory; Waiver of Subrogation |
| **Workers' Compensation** | Statutory limits | Waiver of Subrogation |
| **Employers' Liability** | $1M / $1M / $1M | — |
| **Commercial Auto** | $1M CSL | Additional Insured |
| **Umbrella/Excess** | $5M–$25M project-dependent | Follow-form |
| **Professional Liability** | $1M–$5M if design scope | — |
| **Pollution Liability** | $1M–$5M if environmental scope | — |

**COI Review Checklist:**
- Policy dates (no gaps)
- Carrier A.M. Best rating (minimum A- / VII)
- Correct additional insured language
- Primary and non-contributory language
- Waiver of subrogation endorsements
- Adequate limits for proposed contract value

**Surety Bondability Letter:** Treasury-listed surety, dated within 90 days, confirming single project and aggregate capacity. If surety will not provide this letter for the proposed project size — treat as a significant red flag.

### 4.3 Legal, Regulatory, and Reputational History

**AIA A305 Contractor's Qualification Statement disclosures:**
- Active lawsuits, arbitrations, mediations
- Contract defaults or terminations for cause
- Liquidated damages history
- Bankruptcy filings (current or past 7 years)
- Judgments filed against the firm

**Regulatory Compliance:**
- Active trade licenses (all project jurisdictions)
- Corporate good standing certificate
- OSHA citation history and penalties
- Labor law violations (prevailing wage, Davis-Bacon, FLSA)
- Contractor license suspensions or revocations

**Payment Practices Investigation:**
- Mechanic's lien filings against prior projects (public record search)
- UCC filings indicating secured creditors
- D&B or Experian Business Credit Report
- Supplier and sub-subcontractor references

## Part V: Advanced Risk Scoring — Marsh SubSecure / SDI Integration

**Marsh SubSecure** scores subcontractors across 10 financial areas vs. surety underwriting standards:

1. Liquidity (Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, Days of Cash)
2. Working Capital adequacy
3. Underbillings exposure
4. Overbillings management
5. Profitability trends (Gross Margin, Net Margin)
6. Leverage (Debt to Equity, Times Interest Earned)
7. Backlog relative to capacity
8. Accounts Receivable quality and aging
9. Cash flow from operations
10. Overall balance sheet strength and equity trend

**Cost Containment Impact:** Rigorous prequalification directly reduces:
- WC claims that pierce the GC's deductible
- GL claims from subcontractor negligence
- Subcontractor default events that trigger SDI deductibles
- GC's own EMR over time (improving WC premiums)

## Part VI: Complete Data Point Checklist

### Section A: Company Identity
- Legal entity name and all DBA names
- FEIN / IRS Form W-9
- State of incorporation and good standing certificate
- Year founded and years in business under current ownership
- Ownership structure (all owners with >5% interest)
- NAICS code(s) for primary work performed

### Section B: Licensing and Regulatory Compliance
- All active trade licenses (number, state, expiration, certifying agency)
- Specialty certifications (NFPA, EPA, ASME, NCCCO, NICET, NABCEP)
- Union affiliations and CBA status
- OSHA violation history (citations, penalties, abatement status)
- Labor law violation history
- Environmental compliance history

### Section C: Financial Data Points
- Audited or CPA-reviewed financials (last 2–3 fiscal years)
- Most recent interim financials (within 90 days)
- WIP Schedule (current)
- AR and AP aging schedules
- Bank line of credit confirmation
- Bonding capacity letter (single and aggregate)
- Business credit report (D&B, Experian, or Equifax)
- UCC lien search
- Mechanic's lien history

**Financial Ratios to Calculate:**
- Current Ratio (benchmark: ≥ 1.4)
- Quick Ratio (benchmark: ≥ 1.2)
- Days of Cash on Hand (benchmark: ≥ 15 days)
- Underbillings to Working Capital (benchmark: < 15%)
- Gross Profit Margin (benchmark: ≥ 10%)
- Net Profit Margin (benchmark: ≥ 3%)
- Return on Assets (benchmark: ≥ 8%)
- Debt to Equity Ratio (benchmark: ≤ 2.5x)
- Times Interest Earned (benchmark: ≥ 5x)
- Days in Accounts Receivable (benchmark: ≤ 65 days)
- Months in Backlog (benchmark: 3–15 months)
- Backlog to Working Capital (benchmark: ≤ 8x)
- WIP Profit Fade analysis
- Cash to Overbillings ratio

### Section D: Safety Data Points
- EMR for the past 3 years (verified from WC carrier — not self-reported)
- OSHA 300 Logs (3–5 years)
- OSHA 300A Summaries (3–5 years)
- TRIR calculation (benchmark: ≤ 2.0)
- DART Rate calculation (benchmark: ≤ 1.5)
- OSHA citations (past 5 years)
- Fatality history
- Written Safety and Health Program
- Drug and Alcohol Testing Policy
- Safety Officer qualifications

### Section E: Insurance Data Points
- COI with all required endorsements
- CGL policy limits and carrier rating (minimum A- / VII)
- WC policy and carrier rating
- Employers' Liability limits
- Commercial Auto limits
- Excess/Umbrella limits
- Professional Liability (if design-assist)
- Pollution Liability (if environmental)
- All required endorsements verified
- No coverage gaps or lapses confirmed

### Section F: Capacity and Operational Data Points
- Largest successfully completed project (value + description)
- 5–10 most relevant completed projects with GC references
- Annual revenue (past 3 years)
- Current backlog (value + active project descriptions)
- Total headcount (field and office)
- Labor sourcing strategy
- Key personnel resumes (PM, Superintendent)
- Equipment schedule
- Quality Management Plan

### Section G: Character and Reputation Data Points
- Litigation disclosure (active and past 5 years)
- Default and termination history
- Bankruptcy history (past 7 years)
- Judgment history
- Reference checks (minimum 3 GC references, called directly)
- Supplier and sub-subcontractor references
- Banking relationship reference
- Surety relationship history and current bondability status
