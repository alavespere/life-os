# ICP Strategy — Ideal Customer Profile
_BuildSafe IQ Inc. — Last updated: 2026-04-06_

---

## The Core Thesis
Companies that tightly define and operationalize their ICP outperform across every metric.

Tight ICP = 50% less spend on sales and marketing, 24% shorter CAC payback, 425% higher expansion in year 1.

"Operationalize" means embedding ICP into sales, marketing, onboarding, and customer success — not just writing it on a slide.

---

## The Central Insight
**Use case fit predicts success more accurately than company size, industry, or persona.**

The best customers buy for a core use case your product is built to solve. Products work well for 2–4 key use cases. Beyond that, performance degrades.

A 10-minute conversation about a prospect's intended use case can often predict long-term retention or churn.

---

## BuildSafe IQ ICP

### Firmographic Profile
- **Company type:** Commercial GC (general contractor)
- **Revenue:** $50M–$500M annual construction volume
- **Geography:** Initial focus: NH / New England → expand to target metros
- **Project types:** Commercial, mixed-use, public sector, institutional

### Behavioral Profile (Use Case Fit)
The prospect is a strong ICP if they have at least one of these use cases:

1. **Margin protection:** Lost money on a project due to an uninsured or underinsured risk event in the past 24 months
2. **Subcontractor risk:** Managing 5+ subcontractors on active projects; struggles with sub qualification and financial vetting
3. **Insurance cost pressure:** TCOR increasing year-over-year without understanding why
4. **Proactive compliance:** Under pressure from owners/lenders to demonstrate risk management rigor

### Disqualifying Signals
- Pure residential builder (wrong risk profile)
- Annual construction volume under $25M (risk complexity too low)
- No one at the company owns risk management as a function
- Decision-maker not accessible (large corporation with procurement committee)

---

## ICP Operationalization

### Sales
- Discovery call: qualify use case first, company size second
- Demo: tailored to the use case, not a generic product walkthrough
- Three demo archetypes mapped to the top 3 use cases above

### CRM
- Tag every account: ICP / Non-ICP / Borderline
- Track close rate, deal size, and time-to-close separately by ICP status
- Only count ICP-aligned pipeline in revenue forecast

### Onboarding
- Onboarding pathway differs by use case (margin protection ≠ sub risk ≠ TCOR)
- Standard onboarding kills value realization; personalization drives it

### Success Metrics
- Time to first risk insight delivered: target < 14 days
- 12-month expansion rate: target 40%+
- Referral rate: target 1 referral per 3 clients by month 12

## Framework: Target Market vs. ICP vs. Persona

| Layer | Definition | BuildSafe IQ |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Target Market | Everyone we *could* sell to | Commercial construction $25M+ |
| ICP | The bullseye — fast value, expand, refer | GCs $50M–$500M with active risk exposure |
| Persona | Specific buyer inside ICP companies | CEO, CFO, Risk Manager, Project Executive |

**Key mistake to avoid:** Defining ICP for marketing quantity instead of sales quality. Broad = expensive and slow. Narrow = compound growth.
