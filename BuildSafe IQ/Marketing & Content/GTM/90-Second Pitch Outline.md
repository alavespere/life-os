# 90-Second Pitch Video Outline
_BuildSafe IQ Inc. — Last updated: 2026-04-06_

---

## Structure

### 1. Hook (0:00–0:10)
Drop a hard stat. No warmup.

> "Construction is the riskiest business in the world. 91.5% of projects run over budget or behind schedule — not because contractors are bad at their jobs, but because the risk was invisible until it was too late."

---

### 2. The Problem (0:10–0:40)
The industry hasn't changed in 40 years.

- Teams operating in silos — Procore, Sage, spreadsheets, email. None of them talking to each other.
- Project teams buried in disconnected data. The C-suite finds out what's wrong when it hits the P&L.
- By then: delays, rework, litigation. Margin gone.
- The industry doesn't need another dashboard. It needs someone who understands the chaos — and has lived it.

---

### 3. The Team (0:40–0:55)
Credibility, fast.

- Our team has walked, managed, and built complex projects. We've negotiated coverage, managed claims, and helped GCs deliver billions in construction value.
- Technology lead: scaled enterprise platforms, built API infrastructure for Fortune 500s.
- Data architect: fintech background, turns complex data into actionable intelligence.
- Advisors: former PMs, underwriters, and risk engineers from top-tier firms.

We've lived the problem. Now we've built the team and the technology to solve it.

---

### 4. The Solution (0:55–1:20)
What BuildSafe IQ does.

- Turns live, siloed data into a predictive risk platform
- Aggregates project data to flag high-cost risks early
- Combines historic trends, AI modeling, and expert-backed risk analysis
- No manual data entry — integrates with existing systems
- Output: risk intelligence that protects margin before the project goes sideways

---

### 5. The Ask (1:20–1:30)
Clear, confident, no fluff.

> "We're ready to scale with the right partners. If you're building in construction and tired of finding out what went wrong after the fact — let's talk."

---

## Delivery Notes
- Pace: measured, not rushed
- Tone: practitioner authority — not a startup pitch, a solution announcement
- No corporate language — speak like someone who has been on a job site
- Lead with the problem, not the product
