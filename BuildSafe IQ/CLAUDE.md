## Brand Voice (Non-Negotiable)
_Full guide lives in `Marketing & Content/Content/Voice & Style Guide.md` — read it before drafting any content._

- Style model: Johnny Harris — narrative-first, data-backed, tension-building, one thesis per piece
- Audience: Commercial GCs, subcontractors, developers, and investors
- Tone: Direct, confident, data-grounded. Not academic. Not corporate.
- Reading level: 8th grade
- Short, declarative sentences for emphasis
- Always identify the non-obvious, contrarian angle
- **NEVER use:** "I'm excited to share", listicles, "5 things you should know", corporate passive voice
- **NEVER use:** "delve into", "it's worth noting", "game-changer", "leverage synergies", "in conclusion"
- Always cite sources. Never fabricate data or statistics. Flag uncertain claims as `[VERIFY]`.

## Content Production Rules

1. Read `Marketing & Content/Content/Voice & Style Guide.md` before drafting any content piece
2. Every content piece maps to one of the Five Pillars below
3. Content hierarchy: Research Brief → Pillar Content → Video Script → Article → LinkedIn → Newsletter → Short Posts
4. Include bracketed production notes in video scripts:
   - `[B-ROLL: description]` for footage needs
   - `[GRAPHIC: description]` for data visualizations
   - `[MAP: description]` for geographic visuals
5. Blog and article headlines: specific, data-driven, or contrarian — never vague
6. Lead with the most important thing. Never bury the lede.

### The Five Content Pillars
| # | Pillar | Theme | Audience Pain |
|---|--------|-------|---------------|
| 1 | The Risk Landscape | Regional market analysis, macro drivers, tipping points | "I don't know what's coming and how it affects my projects." |
| 2 | The Contract Battlefield | Liability transfer, red flag clauses, coverage gaps | "I sign contracts I don't fully understand." |
| 3 | The Human Factor | Labor, subcontractor management, safety culture | "My biggest risk is the people on my job site." |
| 4 | The Insurance Maze | Coverage structures, OCIP/CCIP, claims management | "I'm paying for insurance I don't understand." |
| 5 | The Future of Risk | Technology, AI, climate change, regulatory shifts | "The industry is changing faster than I can adapt." |

### Johnny Harris Video Framework
| Segment | Timing | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| The Hook | 0:00–0:30 | Provocative statement or surprising stat. Never "Today we're talking about..." |
| The Problem Setup | 0:30–2:00 | Core tension with specific, concrete examples |
| The Deep Dive | 2:00–12:00 | Systematic exploration. Each section answers a question raised by the previous one. |
| The Synthesis | 12:00–14:00 | The non-obvious insight the audience could not reach without your expertise |
| The CTA | 14:00–15:00 | One specific, actionable takeaway. Not generic advice. |

4. Never extrapolate beyond what the source states
